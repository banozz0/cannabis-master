# 01 — Genetics & Strains

How to choose, evaluate, hunt, and preserve cannabis genetics. Picks up where `00-fundamentals.md` ended (taxonomy, chemovars, biosynthesis) and goes operational on cultivar selection. Cross-reference with `11-propagation.md` (seed germination, clones, mother management), `12-flowering.md` (cultivar-specific stretch and finish times), `18-breeding.md` (making your own crosses), and `19-tissue-culture.md` (HLVd cleanup).

1. **Choosing a cultivar for the next grow** → start at *Strain Stability Spectrum* and *Seed Types Available* to match seed type to your goal, then *Reading Strain Descriptions Critically* before buying.
2. **Running a seed pack and looking for a keeper** → jump to *Pheno-Hunting Protocol*.

## Core Concepts

| Concept | What it means | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Genetics is the ceiling, environment is the floor** | A bad environment ruins a great cultivar; a great environment can't fix bad genetics | Stop trying to fix a herm-prone cultivar with environment — cull the genetics |
| **Strain names mostly do not predict genetic identity** | Sawler et al. 2015 found only moderate correlation between strain-name claims and actual genome | Two packs of "OG Kush" from different breeders may share <50% genome |
| **The modern catalog is highly polyhybrid** | Almost everything is a cross of crosses of crosses, mostly unstable | Expect significant phenotype variation within a pack, even within "F1" claims |
| **Clones inherit genetics AND viroids/viruses AND age** | A clone is a tissue continuation, not a reset | HLVd-positive mothers spread to every cut; clones from flowering plants may pre-flower |
| **Chemovar > morphology** | Cannabinoid + terpene profile describes the plant more reliably than indica/sativa labels | Treat COAs and breeder data as more informative than vibes-based strain labels |

## Landrace Origins

**Landraces** are wild or semi-domesticated populations stabilized by long isolation in a specific geography. They form the genetic foundation of all modern cultivars.

### Major Landrace Regions

| Region | Climate niche | What it contributed | Notable derivatives |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hindu Kush, Afghanistan** | High-altitude, arid, short summer | Broad-leaf drug (BLD) morphology, dense buds, fast finish, hash production tradition, resin glands | Afghani #1, Hash Plant, Northern Lights base |
| **Pakistan / Chitral** | Mountain valleys | BLD with "chitrali" terpene profile | Chitral Kush, Pakistani Chitral |
| **Thailand** | Equatorial, year-round growing | Narrow-leaf drug (NLD), 14+ week flowering, soaring effects, high terpinolene | Thai Stick, Thai-derived Haze parents |
| **Colombia** | Equatorial Andes | NLD, classic Colombian "punto rojo" red-pistil expression | Colombian Gold, Punto Rojo |
| **Mexico** | Mid-latitude, dry summer | NLD, fast flowering for an NLD, classic 70s-era smoke | Acapulco Gold, Oaxacan |
| **South Africa** | Subtropical | NLD with unique terpene profile, autoflower-like fast finish in some lines | Durban Poison, Swazi Gold |
| **Malawi** | Tropical highlands | NLD, very long flowering, intense Haze-type effects | Malawi Gold |
| **Nepal / Northern India** | Charas-tradition mountain regions | BLD with hashy / floral profile | Nepalese, Manali Cream |
| **Russia / Central Asia** | Short summer, high latitudes | Ruderalis — small, weedy, age-based flowering | Every modern autoflower |
| **Jamaica** | Tropical island | NLD, often Colombian-derived secondary populations | Lambs Bread |

### Landrace Preservation
True landraces are increasingly rare — modern hybrids have crossed back into landrace regions, diluting the gene pools. Preservation projects:
- **The Real Seed Co.** — focused on Hindu Kush and Himalayan landraces (Tier 3 — community-respected).
- **Khalifa Genetics** — Moroccan and African landraces.
- **ACE Seeds, Cannabiogen** — landrace × landrace working lines.

If a "landrace" pack costs the same as a polyhybrid and ships from a generic seed bank, treat the claim with skepticism. Real landrace work is small-batch and slow.

## The Hybridization Era

Every modern commercial cultivar traces back to a small set of foundational hybrids. Understanding the lineage clarifies what's actually being sold.

### Foundational Crosses (1970s–1990s)

```
Skunk #1 (Sam the Skunkman, 1970s)
  = Colombian Gold × Acapulco Gold × Afghan
  → first stable indica/sativa hybrid; the parent of much of modern cannabis

Northern Lights (Sensi Seeds line)
  = Afghani-dominant indica
  → fast flowering, dense bud — backbone of countless crosses

Haze (original Haze Brothers, Santa Cruz)
  = Mexican × Colombian × South Indian × Thai
  → long-flowering NLD; parent of Super Silver Haze, Amnesia, etc.

Original Diesel / Chemdog
  = NYC/East Coast lineage, parents disputed
  → progenitor of the entire Diesel and Sour family
```

### The OG Kush → Cookies → Gelato Tree (2000s–2010s)

This is one dominant lineage in the modern polyhybrid catalog.

```

  ├── Triangle Kush, SFV OG, Tahoe OG, Ghost OG (clone-only cuts)
  └── Bred into → Girl Scout Cookies (GSC, San Francisco mid-2000s)
                    = OG Kush × Durban Poison (Cookies Fam)
                    │
                    ├── Sunset Sherbet (GSC × Pink Panties)
                    │     │
                    │     └── Gelato (Mr. Sherbinski / Cookies Fam, 2014)
                    │           = Sunset Sherbet × Thin Mint GSC
                    │           │
                    │           └── Wedding Cake = Triangle Kush × Animal Mints
```

## Strain Stability Spectrum

Not all "seeds of strain X" are equal. The genetic stability of what's in the pack drives how uniform your phenos will be.

### F1 Hybrid (First Cross)
- **Definition:** Cross of two distinct parents (usually inbred lines or stable cultivars).
- **Vigor:** High — F1 hybrid vigor (heterosis) makes F1s often the most vigorous version of a cross.
- **Uniformity:** Reasonably uniform within a pack IF both parents are stable lines. If the parents themselves are polyhybrids (common today), "F1" is misleading marketing.
- **When to use:** Production runs where you want predictability and vigor. True F1s of stable parents are the safest bet.
- **When to avoid:** Don't expect uniformity from "F1" packs whose parents are themselves hybrids.

### F2 (Sibling Cross from F1)
- **Definition:** F1 × F1 from the same generation. Mendelian segregation makes F2 highly variable.
- **Vigor:** Variable — some F2s show vigor, many show recessive trait expression.
- **Uniformity:** Low — this is the generation breeders run to find recessive traits and rare expressions.
- **When to use:** Pheno-hunting for keepers; breeding work; finding outliers (huge or tiny, fast or slow, unique terps).
- **When to avoid:** Production runs where uniformity matters.

### IBL (Inbred Line)
- **Definition:** Multiple generations of inbreeding (F4, F5, F6+) selecting for stability around a single phenotype.
- **Vigor:** Reduced — inbreeding depression caps vigor.
- **Uniformity:** High — the point of an IBL.
- **When to use:** Breeding parent for new F1 crosses; preserving a specific phenotype as seed stock.
- **When to avoid:** As a production cultivar — vigor loss usually means lower yield than F1 versions.

### BX (Backcross)
- **Definition:** F1 × Original Parent (BX1), then BX1 × Original Parent (BX2), etc. Recovers the original parent's traits while keeping a small genetic contribution from the other side.
- **Vigor:** Moderate, declines with successive BX generations.
- **Uniformity:** Increases with each backcross.
- **When to use:** When you want a specific clone-only mother turned into a seed line — backcross the clone repeatedly to lock its traits into seed-distributable form.
- **When to avoid:** Don't expect the BX line to fully replace the clone — it's an approximation.

### S1 (Selfed — Feminized Seed from a Single Mother)
- **Definition:** A female plant is reversed (chemically forced to produce pollen with silver thiosulfate or colloidal silver) and pollinated with its own pollen. Offspring are 100% female.
- **Vigor:** Variable, often reduced from the parent due to inbreeding via selfing.
- **Uniformity:** Low to moderate — Mendelian selfing exposes recessives the parent was hiding.
- **When to use:** Preserving a clone-only cut as feminized seed; producing seed when only one mother exists.
- **When to avoid:** S1s of polyhybrid parents are extremely variable — don't expect a "Gelato 33 S1" to all be Gelato 33.

### Polyhybrid
- **Definition:** Crosses of crosses of crosses, with no inbred-line stabilization. The vast majority of modern packs.
- **Vigor:** Variable.
- **Uniformity:** Low — every plant is meaningfully different.
- **When to use:** When you're pheno-hunting and want variation. Most "designer" packs (Runtz crosses, etc.) are polyhybrids.
- **When to avoid:** Production runs that need consistency across plants.

## Seed Types Available

| Type | What it is | When to choose | Trade-offs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Regular** | Both male and female possible (~50/50) | Breeding, seed-making, finding keepers from a pack with male contribution | Have to sex plants; lose half to males in non-breeding contexts |
| **Feminized** | All offspring female | Production grows, photoperiod harvests | Slightly higher herm risk in stress; not for breeding without reversal |
| **Autoflower** | Ruderalis-derived; flowers by age | Multiple harvests per year, stealth, beginners, no light schedule changes | Smaller plants, lower per-plant yield, no useful clones |
| **Fast / Quick** | Photoperiod × autoflower cross, then selected for shorter flowering — still photoperiod-triggered | Outdoor harvests in short-summer climates | Flowering time shorter than parent photo, but vigor and yield often reduced |
| **CBD-dominant** | Selected Type III (CBD-dominant) chemovar | Therapeutic / low-THC products | Lower THC than commercial cultivars; flowering and care identical to standard photo |
| **High-CBG / Type IV** | Engineered to express CBG with little THC/CBD | CBG product market | Niche; cultivation identical to photo regular |

## Pheno-Hunting Protocol

Pheno-hunting is the process of running a seed pack, evaluating individual plants, and selecting the best for cloning and continued use ("the keeper"). The math: more seeds = better odds of finding a true outlier.

### Pop Size Math

Realistic pop-size guidance (Tier 3 — pheno-hunt forum and breeder consensus):

| Pop size | Realistic outcome |
|---|---|
| 1–5 seeds | Likely all average for the pack; no outliers expected |
| 10–20 seeds | One or two stand-outs likely; enough for most home growers |
| 50–100 seeds | Reasonable chance of a true keeper if the pack has one to give |
| 100–500 seeds | Serious commercial / breeder hunt; expect to find the cream |
| 1,000+ seeds | Industrial pheno-hunt; finding a generational keeper |

**Diminishing returns:** popping 1000 seeds doesn't 100× a 10-seed pop's chances of a keeper. The breeder's selection at the seed level caps the upper bound. A weak pack at 1000 still won't yield what a strong pack does at 50.

### Selection Stages

**Stage 1 — Sprout vigor (week 1–2 from sprout)**
- Speed of germination and emergence
- Cotyledon size and color
- Speed of true-leaf development
- Cull obvious runts and slow starters unless you have unlimited space

**Stage 2 — Vegetative structure (weeks 2–6 of veg)**
- Internodal spacing — tight (preferred for indoor) vs stretched
- Branching structure — bushy / candelabra / leader-dominant
- Leaf shape and color (chlorosis or oddities)
- Vigor under topping/training stress
- Smell on stem rub — strong aromatic stems often correlate with strong terps later

**Stage 3 — Stretch and structure (flower weeks 1–3)**
- Stretch ratio (does it double, triple, more?) — informs which space it fits
- Bud site count
- Pre-flower smell intensity
- Herm risk visible? (Cull immediately if true intersex appears.)

**Stage 4 — Bud development (weeks 3–6 of flower)**
- Bud structure (dense / fluffy / golf-ball / column)
- Pistil density and color
- Trichome production rate (visible at week 4–5 if early producer)
- Smell evolution — does it stay strong, fade, change?

**Stage 5 — Ripening (weeks 6+)**
- Finish time vs pack-claim
- Bract swelling
- Trichome amber-up curve
- Late-stage herm risk

**Stage 6 — Cure (4+ weeks post-dry)**
- Smell preservation — does the cured nose hold up?
- Smoke / vapor smoothness, ash color, taste
- Effect profile on you specifically

### Tagging System

Number every plant. Same number through veg, flower, harvest, dry, cure. Per-plant notebook entries (or grow-log entries — see `state/grow-log.md`):

```
Plant #03:
- vigor: high
- stretch: 2.2x
- structure: leader with 4 strong sides
- nose veg: gas + diesel
- nose week 4: gas + diesel + faint cake
- nose cure week 2: gas + cake, no hay
- yield: 142g dry
- finish: day 63
- decision: KEEPER — clone before chop
```

### Cull Triggers

Immediate cull (don't waste space):
- Hermaphrodite or intersex (anthers in female flower) — genetic herm is heritable
- Severe weakness / chronic disease vulnerability
- Slow finisher with no compensating quality (lower yield AND poor terps)

Conditional cull (depends on goals):
- Average everything — keeper is the *outlier*, not the median
- Yield-weak with great terps — keep if connoisseur focus, cull if production
- Yield-strong with mid terps — keep if commercial volume, cull if quality focus

### Keeper Preservation

Once a keeper is identified:
- **Take clones BEFORE flower week 4** — easiest cuts; flowering clones are harder to root and pre-flowered.
- **Tissue-culture backup** if you have the means — see `19-tissue-culture.md`. A keeper lost to HLVd or pest die-off is a real loss.
- **Document everything** in grow-log: lineage, pack name, breeder, hunt date, distinguishing features.
- **Mother-plant management** — see `11-propagation.md`.

## Clone-Only Cultivars and HLVd

**Clone-only** means a cultivar exists only as a vegetative cutting line — no seed version is widely available, or the available seed versions don't replicate the clone's expression. Examples: GG #4 (formerly "Gorilla Glue #4"), the original Cookies cuts, Triangle Kush, Forum Cookies.

Clone-only cuts circulate hand-to-hand, through clone programs, nurseries, and private grower networks. The risk: **viroids and viruses transfer with every cut.**

### The HLVd Crisis

**Hop Latent Viroid (HLVd / HpLVd)** is a small, infectious RNA molecule that infects cannabis (and hops, for which it was named). It causes "dudding" — slow growth, low yield, low potency, weak smell, brittle stems. Often misdiagnosed as nutrient or environmental issues for years before being identified.

Key facts (Tier 1 — Bektas et al., Adkar-Purushothama; Tier 2 — Dark Heart Nursery 2018–2021 survey, ~$4 billion industry-wide loss estimated):

- **~90% of California cultivation facilities tested positive** for HLVd in the Dark Heart 2018–2021 survey of 200,000+ tissue samples.
- **Symptoms are non-obvious early** — infected plants may look fine until flower stage, where yield and quality collapse.
- **Spreads via:**
  - Mechanical (unclean tools — pruners, scissors)
  - Sap/water (irrigation re-use, shared trays)
  - Vegetative (clones from infected mothers — every clone is infected)
  - Possibly seed (low rate; Tier 1 evidence still emerging)
  - Insects (low rate)
- **Asymptomatic infection is the norm** — you cannot scout HLVd visually with confidence. PCR test is the only reliable diagnostic.

### Sourcing Clean Cuts

1. **Buy from nurseries that test for HLVd** — Dark Heart, Node Labs, Phylos, others publish their testing protocols. Demand current test results before paying.
2. **Quarantine new arrivals** — minimum 2-week isolation before introducing to mother stock. Consider PCR testing the new arrival.
3. **Tissue culture cleanup** — meristem culture can clean an infected line. See `19-tissue-culture.md`. Specialty labs offer this as a service.
4. **Sterilize tools between plants** — bleach (10% solution), Virkon S, or flame. Especially critical when handling multiple cultivars.
5. **Don't share clones casually** — if you trade with another grower, both gardens are now linked epidemiologically.

If you've had a "weak phenotype that should be stronger," declining mother-plant performance over time, or suddenly low-yielding runs without environment changes — get HLVd testing before assuming it's anything else.

## Seed Banks — How to Evaluate

Seed bank quality varies enormously. Red flags and green flags:

### Green Flags
- **Long track record** with the same name (5+ years).
- **Stable lineup** — packs they listed 3 years ago are still listed.
- **In-house breeding work**, not just reselling other breeders.
- **Published parent lineage** for every pack (not just "Indica Hybrid").
- **Test grower / pheno-hunt photos** from real grows, dated, with COA when relevant.
- **Customer-service responsiveness** — replies to support emails within reasonable time.
- **Reasonable germination guarantees** with replacement policy.
- **Discreet shipping** with stealth options.

### Red Flags
- **Brand-new "premium" bank with all the famous names** for sale — likely reselling unauthenticated seeds.
- **Stock photos only**, no actual grow images.
- **No lineage information** on packs.
- **Generic "feminized photoperiod" labels** with no breeder credit.
- **Suspiciously low prices** for hyped genetics — likely fakes (Cookies Fam, Seed Junky, Compound packs cost what they cost; deep discounts are usually counterfeit).
- **Aggressive coupons / dropshipping site signals** — many fly-by-night sites repackage low-quality seed.

### Established Banks and Breeders Worth Knowing

Tier 3 / community-respected (no endorsement, just commonly cited):

| Category | Names |
|---|---|
| Foundational European banks | Sensi Seeds, Dutch Passion, Royal Queen Seeds, Greenhouse, Serious Seeds, Paradise |
| Mid-era European | Mr. Nice (Shantibaba), Soma Seeds, ACE Seeds, Cannabiogen |
| North American craft (since 2010s) | Bodhi Seeds, Mass Medical Strains, Cannarado, Exotic Genetix, In House, Compound, Tiki Madman, Solfire, Capulator, Skunkmasterflex |
| Cookies / Gelato lineage holders | Cookies Fam, Sherbinski, Seed Junky |
| Specialty / landrace | The Real Seed Co., Khalifa Genetics, Snowhigh, ACE Seeds |
| Autoflower | Mephisto Genetics, Fast Buds, Royal Queen autos |

Buying from authorized retailers of these breeders matters. The retailer is the chain-of-custody guarantee.

### Tester Programs

Many breeders run **tester programs** — free or discounted packs in exchange for documented grow reports. Tester reports are a valuable signal when evaluating a new release: lots of testers reporting consistent positive results = a stable line. Few testers, or only positive testers showcased = marketing.

## Reading Strain Descriptions Critically

Translate breeder marketing into reality:

| Breeder claim | Likely reality |
|---|---|
| "Up to 30% THC" | A single test of one outlier pheno in optimal conditions; expect 18–24% on average |
| "Highest yield ever" | Marketing copy — not predictive. Trust grow reports, not breeder claims |
| "8-week flower" | Often the fast-finish pheno; mid-pack often 9–10 weeks |
| "Heavy producer / 600g/m²" | Best-case under optimal indoor; expect 300–450g/m² real-world |
| "Indica-dominant 70/30" | Morphology guess by breeder; not a reliable effect predictor |
| "Connoisseur grade" | No specific meaning; marketing |
| "F1 hybrid" | Often a polyhybrid called F1; check parent stability |
| "Mostly bag-seed of [hyped strain]" | Almost certainly not the hyped strain even if it grew next to it; could be selfed, herm-pollinated, or random |
| "Easy to grow" | Beginner-friendly description, often accurate when paired with mainstream breeder |

### Trust Signals

- Lineage with both parents named, not "Unknown" or "Mystery cross"
- Flowering-time range, not single number
- Breeder's own grow report with photos and timeline
- Independent test-grower reports across multiple climates
- Available COA (Certificate of Analysis) for cannabinoid + terpene profile of the pheno being marketed

## Modern Hype Cycles

The cannabis seed market runs on hype cycles. Knowing where the market is helps you avoid paying premium for what will be commodity in 18 months.

| Era (approx.) | Hype family | Why it ascended | What replaced it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1990s | Skunk, Northern Lights, Haze | Foundational — first mass-popularity strains | OG Kush emergence |
| Early 2000s | OG Kush family (West Coast) | West Coast clone/culture scene | Cookies family |
| 2010–2015 | Girl Scout Cookies family | New flavor profile (sweet/dessert) | Gelato |
| 2015–2020 | Gelato, Wedding Cake, Sherbet | Cookies refinement; Mr. Sherbinski / Cookies Fam dominance | Runtz / Z-line |
| 2020–2025 | Runtz, Zkittlez crosses, "candy terps" | Sweet/fruity profile dominance | TBD — current cycle still cresting |
| 2025+ | Hash-line breeding, terpene-isolate cultivars, "ice cream" lines | Terp-focused selection driven by hash market growth | Forming now |

**Practical takeaway:** the hyped strain of today is the mid-tier strain of three years from now. If you're hunting for a long-term keeper, lineage stability and your own evaluation matter more than current hype.

## Confusion / Misdiagnosis Traps

| Looks like | Actually | How to tell |
|---|---|---|
| "OG Kush" from random bank | Almost certainly not the original OG cut; modern S1s and crosses share little with the clone | Real OG cuts are tightly held; bulk seed "OG" is hybrid |
| Identical-looking pack mates → uniform F1 | Polyhybrid sold as F1 | If both parents are themselves polyhybrids, F1 claim is meaningless |
| "Stable strain" because all grow similar | Single grow conditions can mask genetic variation | Run the same pack twice in different rooms — variation appears |
| Auto runt is "weak genetics" | Most autoflower runts are stress-induced (light early, transplant shock) | Other autos in the same pack at full vigor → environment, not genetics |
| Slow weak grower is genetic | HLVd asymptomatic infection | PCR test; if positive, the genetics may be fine after tissue-culture cleanup |
| "Indica" effect | Chemovar effect | COA tells the truth; shelf labels do not |
| Clone "fast pheno" | Just an early-rooted clone with more veg time before flip | Standardize veg time across clones to compare phenos fairly |
| Pheno difference because "different cuts" | Same clone, different age or environment | Same genetic source ≠ same expression unless mother conditions match |
| "Reverse-engineered Gelato 33 S1" | S1s diverge from the parent significantly; Gelato 33 specifically is hard to S1 stably | Trust only seed work from breeders with documented S1 protocols |
| New breeder "killing the game" with cheap hyped names | Often counterfeits or untested polyhybrids with hyped parent names | 5+ year track record + customer reports = real |

## Quick Reference

### Decision Matrix — Which Seed Type for Which Goal?

| Goal | Best seed type | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Reliable production grow, single-strain crop | F1 of stable IBL parents, OR proven clone | Uniformity for SOPs |
| Quick turnaround, multiple harvests/year | Autoflower | 8–12 week cycle, no light schedule changes |
| Pheno-hunt for a personal keeper | Polyhybrid pack, 20–50 seeds | Variation increases keeper odds |
| Preserve a clone you have | S1 (selfed) seed run | Replicates approximate genetics for storage |
| Breeding stock | Regular seeds (need males) | Half male / half female required for crossing |
| Outdoor short summer | Autoflower or Fast/Quick photo | Fits short window |
| Therapeutic, low-THC | CBD-dominant Type III | Selected chemovar |
| First-ever grow | Beginner-friendly photo F1, well-documented | Predictability + community support |

### Pheno-Hunt Pop-Size Reference

| Goal | Recommended pack count |
|---|---|
| Hobby / personal supply | 1–3 packs (10–30 seeds) |
| Commercial keeper hunt | 5–10 packs (50–100 seeds) |
| Serious breeder release work | 10–50+ packs (100–500+ seeds) |
| Industrial / generational keeper | 1,000+ seeds |
