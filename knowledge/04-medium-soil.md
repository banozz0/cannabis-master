# 04 — Medium: Soil

Soil-grown cannabis with synthetic, organic-bottle, or amended-organic feeding. Soil is the most forgiving medium — the buffer slows mistakes and gives the grower recovery time. **For no-till living-soil cultivation, see `04-medium-living-soil.md` instead** (different mental model, biology-driven). Cross-reference with `04-medium-coco.md` and `04-medium-hydro.md` (when comparing mediums), `05-water-quality.md` (input water + alkalinity behavior in soil), `06-nutrients-synthetic.md` (feed schedule plug-in), `06-nutrients-organic.md` (organic top-dress and tea options for soil), `07-deficiencies.md` (soil-specific lockout patterns).

## How to Use This File

Diagnostic order for any soil problem:

1. **Soil moisture first** — over- or underwatering causes more "deficiencies" than actual nutrient issues
2. **pH second** — runoff or slurry test; soil drift is slow but real
3. **EC third** — runoff vs input tells you about salt buildup
4. **Mix evaluation** — is the mix appropriate for the stage?
5. **Feed schedule** — is feeding starting too early, too late, or wrong strength for the mix?

## Core Concepts

| Concept | What it means | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Soil is a buffered medium** | The mix holds reserves of nutrients, water, and pH stability | Mistakes take days to manifest, not hours; corrections also take days |
| **Wet/dry cycle is the bedrock** | Roots need oxygen; saturated soil suffocates them | The single biggest soil killer is overwatering |
| **pH range 6.0–7.0, target 6.3–6.8** | Wider sweet spot than coco/hydro | Less critical to chase decimals; hard limits matter more than exact target |
| **Amended soil feeds itself first** | Hot mixes carry weeks of nutrition; light mixes carry none | Feeding too early on hot soil = nute burn; too late on light mix = deficiency |
| **Recovery time is days, not hours** | Slower than coco/hydro; less catastrophic when wrong | Trust the medium, don't panic-react to one off reading |
| **Containers determine drainage and root health** | Same soil + different container = different watering frequency, different yield | Pot type and size are upstream of every watering decision |

## Soil Composition Basics

A cannabis soil mix is engineered for **water retention + drainage + nutrition**. Components and their roles:

| Component | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Sphagnum peat moss** | Water retention base, mild acidity | Most common base in commercial mixes; dolomite added to buffer the acidity |
| **Coco coir** | Water retention + better drainage than peat | Common alternative or supplement to peat in modern mixes |
| **Perlite** | Aeration, drainage, prevents compaction | 20–30% by volume in most cannabis-suitable mixes |
| **Pumice / vermiculite** | Aeration alternative; vermiculite holds water more | Less common in cannabis mixes than perlite |
| **Compost** | Slow-release nutrition + biological activity | Quality varies dramatically — bagged "compost" is often peat-coloured woodchips |
| **Worm castings** | Mild N + P + biology | Workhorse organic amendment; rarely overdoes anything |
| **Bat guano** | High P (bloom guanos) or high N (Mexican bat guano) | Strong amendment, easy to overdo |
| **Steamed bone meal** | P + Ca, slow release | Long-term P source for full-cycle organic |
| **Blood meal** | High N, fast release | Easy to burn with; common Subcool-style amendment |
| **Rock phosphate** | Slow-release P over months | Set-and-forget P amendment |
| **Dolomite lime ("sweet lime")** | Ca + Mg + raises pH | Buffers peat-acid mixes; standard amendment |
| **Oyster shell flour** | Slow-release Ca + alkaline buffer | Often combined with dolomite |
| **Gypsum** | Ca + S without raising pH | Useful where pH is already correct but Ca/S are low |
| **Azomite / glacial rock dust** | Trace minerals (60+) | Insurance against micronutrient gaps |
| **Mycorrhizal inoculant** | Root symbiosis | Most pre-made cannabis mixes already include; modest benefit on cannabis (weak mycotrophic species) |
| **Humic acid / fulvic acid** | Chelation, microbial food, micro mobility | Common in modern hot mixes |

## Pre-Made Soil Mixes (Ranked by "Hot")

Mixes range from inert (you feed everything) to fully amended (mix feeds itself for weeks). Pick by stage and grower preference.

### Light / Inert (You Feed from Day 1)

| Mix | What's in it | Feed required from |
|---|---|---|
| **Pro-Mix HP** | Peat + perlite + dolomite + mycos. No real nutrition. | Day 1 |
| **Sunshine Mix #4** | Similar to Pro-Mix HP | Day 1 |
| **Coco-perlite blend** | Inert organic-feeling alternative | Day 1 — but treat per `04-medium-coco.md` |

**Use case:** growers who want full feed-schedule control; clones and seedlings; commercial soil-less production wanting consistency.

### Medium-Amended (Self-Feeds 1–2 Weeks)

| Mix | What's in it | Feed required from |
|---|---|---|
| **Fox Farm Happy Frog** | Sphagnum + perlite + earthworm castings + bat guano + oyster shell + dolomite + mycos. Mild. | Week 2–3 |
| **Roots Organics Original** | Sphagnum + perlite + bat guano + worm castings + glacial dust. Light enough for seedlings. | Week 2 |
| **Coast of Maine Bar Harbor Blend** | New England-style with kelp, lobster compost, dolomite | Week 2 |

**Use case:** the safe default for most growers — won't burn seedlings, gives an easy 2-week buffer before feed schedule starts.

### Heavily Amended ("Hot" — Self-Feeds 3–6 Weeks)

| Mix | What's in it | Feed required from | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fox Farm Ocean Forest** | Sphagnum + perlite + forest humus + bat guano + crab + fish meal + earthworm castings | Week 4–6 | Will burn seedlings and clones; up-pot from a lighter mix |
| **Coast of Maine Stonington Blend** | Lobster compost + worm castings + alfalfa + kelp + greensand | Week 4–6 | Premium-priced |
| **Roots Organics Greenfields** | Hotter Roots variant | Week 4–6 | Same caveats — not for seedlings |

**Use case:** transplant into Ocean Forest or similar at the 1-gal stage from a lighter mix; minimal feeding through veg.

### Super-Loaded (Self-Feeds Most/All of Cycle — Approaching Living-Soil)

| Mix | What's in it | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **Build-A-Soil 3.0 (BAS)** | Coots-style biological mix + craft compost + amendments | Top-dress only; treat closer to living soil — see `04-medium-living-soil.md` |
| **KIS Organics Tupur Living Soil** | Coco + peat + Coots-style amendments | Same — top-dress only |
| **Subcool Super Soil layered with base** | DIY hot soil layered at bottom, base soil on top | Self-feeds full cycle in 5-gal+ containers |

**Use case:** growers wanting a no-bottle-feeding cycle; transitioning toward true no-till living soil.

## Custom Soil Mixes

### Subcool's Super Soil (the foundational DIY hot mix)

Recipe per ~80 gallons of finished soil (Tier 3 — original Subcool/TGA Genetics recipe):

```
8 × 1.5 cu ft bags of high-quality organic potting soil with coco fiber + mycorrhizae
25–50 lb earthworm castings
5 lb steamed bone meal
5 lb bloom bat guano (high-P)
5 lb blood meal (high-N)
3 lb rock phosphate
3/4 cup Epsom salts (Mg + S)
1/2 cup dolomite/sweet lime (Ca + Mg, pH buffer)
1/2 cup Azomite (trace minerals)
2 tbsp powdered humic acid
```

**Mixing:** combine on a tarp, water lightly, cover with plastic for **30+ days** to "cook" — microbial activity breaks down amendments into plant-available forms. Stir weekly. Smell sweet/earthy = ready; smell sour/ammonia = still cooking.

**Layered approach (the Subcool method):**
1. Bottom 1/3 of finishing pot: layer of cooked super soil
2. Top 2/3: standard base soil (Roots Organics Original, Pro-Mix HP, or similar)
3. Transplant rooted clone into base soil layer
4. Roots grow down through base, hit super soil layer, draw amendments through cycle

Result: full-cycle organic feed with only water + occasional Cal-Mg / supplement adjustments.

### Coots Mix (KIS / craft of soil)

Equal parts (by volume):
- **1/3 peat moss** (or coco coir)
- **1/3 aeration** (perlite, pumice, lava rock)
- **1/3 high-quality compost** (worm castings + composted forest humus + EWC)

Per cubic foot of mix, add:
- 1/2 cup neem seed meal (N + pest deterrent)
- 1/2 cup kelp meal (K + trace + cytokinins)
- 1/2 cup crustacean meal (Ca + chitin → microbial food)
- 1 cup basalt rock dust or glacial dust (trace minerals)
- 1 cup gypsum (Ca + S, pH-neutral)
- 1 cup oyster shell flour (Ca + slow alkaline buffer)

Cook 30–60 days. Use as a no-till medium with ongoing top-dress amendments — see `04-medium-living-soil.md`.

### Build-Your-Own Quick Ratio

For a basic well-drained cannabis mix:

```
60% peat moss OR coco coir (or 30% each)
25% perlite
10% worm castings
5% compost
+ 1 tbsp dolomite per gallon of mix (pH buffer + Ca/Mg)
```

Light mix — feeding required from week 2. Add hot amendments (guanos, blood/bone meal) in small doses if hotter behavior wanted.

## Container Selection

### Sizes by Stage

| Stage | Container size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Seedling (day 1–14) | Solo cup, 0.5–1 gal | Drainage holes critical; transparent solo cups good for watching root development |
| Early veg (week 2–4) | 1–3 gal | First up-pot |
| Late veg (week 4–8+) | 3–7 gal indoor / 10+ gal outdoor | |
| Mature flower (indoor) | 5–10 gal | Sweet spot for most indoor plants |
| Mature flower (outdoor) | 15–30 gal | Larger structure; root mass matters more outdoors |

**Rule of thumb:** ~1 gallon of pot per ~12 inches of expected final plant height for indoor production. Smaller pots force shorter veg + smaller plant; bigger pots reward longer veg + bigger plant.

### Container Type Comparison

| Type | Pros | Cons | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Plastic** | Cheap, reusable, low evaporation, easy to clean | Roots circle, less oxygen at edges, heavier when wet | Cold rooms (slower dry-down is OK), beginners, cheap volume |
| **Fabric (Smart Pots, Root Pouch)** | Air pruning → branching root system, faster dry-down (tighter wet/dry cycle), higher yield potential (20% bump in some studies vs plastic, Tier 2 RQS), reusable | Faster dry-down = water more often, evaporation cools soil (issue in cool rooms), can stain floors | Most indoor cannabis — the modern default |
| **Air pots** | Hard-sided fabric-pot equivalent: air pruning + structure + reusable + easier handling | Higher cost; harder to fit in tight tents | Long-running plants, mother plants, breeders |
| **Smart pots (rigid)** | Hybrid of plastic + air pot — perforated rigid sides | Niche product | Same as air pots |

**Indoor default for most growers:** fabric (Smart Pots or equivalent) at 3 gal for short veg → 5–7 gal for production; 7–15 gal for full-cycle no-up-pot or mother plants.

## Up-Potting / Transplant

### When to Up-Pot
- Roots visible at the bottom drainage holes (key signal)
- Plant getting top-heavy in current container
- Watering frequency dropping below 24 hr in current container

### How (No-Shock Method)
1. Water both source pot and destination soil **24 hr before transplant** (moist but not saturated)
2. Pre-fill destination pot with new soil to leave a hole sized for the source root ball
3. Squeeze sides of source pot (plastic) or peel back fabric — gently invert
4. Catch root ball intact in your hand — don't pull by stem
5. Lower into hole, backfill, lightly press around the edges
6. Water in with mild solution (root stim + mycos optional, plain water fine)
7. **Don't transplant during peak of stretch (flower week 2)** or in late flower

### Container Sequencing
Common indoor sequence:
- Day 1–14: solo cup or 1 gal
- Day 15: → 3 gal
- Day 30 (or before flip): → 5–7 gal final

Skipping intermediate up-pots ("plant in final pot from day 1") is fine for autoflowers (don't tolerate transplant well anyway) and for growers running long veg in big pots — but new soil with no root mass dries slowly and risks overwatering.

## Watering Protocol

### The Wet/Dry Cycle (the bedrock)

Saturate to ~10–20% runoff (synthetic feed) or 0% runoff (organic-only). Wait until pot reaches **50–60% dryback** before next watering. Roots get oxygen during the dry phase — that's how they grow.

### How to Test Dryness

| Method | How | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| **Lift weight** | Pick up the pot — calibrate by weighing fully wet vs fully dry once, learn the feel | Excellent (most reliable for experienced growers) |
| **Finger probe** | Insert finger 2 inches into soil — dry = water | Good for surface, misses deeper saturation |
| **Wood skewer** | Push skewer to bottom, pull out, check moisture along length | Good for diagnosis |
| **Moisture meter (cheap analog)** | Stick in soil, read 1–10 scale | Notoriously inaccurate; use for trend, not absolute |
| **Volumetric water content (VWC) sensor** | Capacitive sensor with reading 0–100% | Excellent — Sensoterra, Acclima, Pulse Pro pucks |
| **Knock test** | Tap pot side — hollow sound = dry, dull = wet | Useful for fabric pots when lifting is hard |

**Best practice:** lift weight + visual top-soil cue. Calibrate once by watering to drainage and weighing, then weighing again 3–5 days later when ready to water again. Learn the wet/dry weight delta for your container size.

### Volume per Watering

| Substrate state | Recommended water volume | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Fully dry | 25–33% of pot volume | Saturate substrate, ~10–20% runoff |
| Damp (rare timing) | 10–15% of pot volume | Maintain moisture without runoff |
| Saturated already | Don't water | Wait |

Examples: 5-gal pot = ~1.25–1.65 gal per watering; 3-gal pot = 0.75–1 gal per watering.

### Timing
- **Lights-on hours, ideally 1–2 hours after lights turn on** — gives plant the photoperiod to use the water and oxygen-replace the substrate
- **Never lights-off** — cold wet roots overnight = root rot risk, especially in fabric pots in cool rooms
- **Never just before lights-off** — same reason

### Watering Frequency by Stage

| Stage | Plant size | Frequency in 5-gal fabric |
|---|---|---|
| Seedling (in solo cup) | Tiny | Every 3–5 days; tiny volume |
| Early veg | Small | Every 3–4 days |
| Mature veg | Medium | Every 2–3 days |
| Flower stretch | Medium-large | Every 2 days |
| Mid flower | Large | Every 1–2 days |
| Late flower | Large + heavy buds | Daily or near-daily |

Plant size + leaf surface area + canopy heat all drive transpiration. A bigger plant in the same pot = more frequent watering. Frequency is not a calendar — it's plant-driven.

## pH and EC for Soil

### pH

- **Acceptable range:** 6.0–7.0 (wider than coco/hydro)
- **Optimal target:** 6.3–6.8
- **Below 5.8 in soil:** Mg, Ca, P, K start locking out
- **Above 7.2 in soil:** P, Fe, Mn, Zn lock out

### Slurry Test (the most reliable substrate pH check)

1. Take 1 part substrate from middle of root zone (avoid top-dressing layer)
2. Mix with 2 parts pH-neutral water (RO or distilled)
3. Stir, let sit 15 minutes
4. Test pH with calibrated probe

This reveals **substrate pH** — what the roots actually experience. Runoff pH is influenced by current input plus what's leaching out, so it lags substrate state by hours-to-days.

### Runoff Reading

Useful but interpret carefully:

| Runoff finding | Likely cause |
|---|---|
| Runoff EC > input EC | Salt buildup in substrate; leach with plain pH'd water (1.5× pot volume) |
| Runoff EC < input EC | Plant absorbing well, no buildup |
| Runoff pH << input pH | Substrate is acidifying — usually from high-N feed or peat aging |
| Runoff pH >> input pH | Substrate is alkalinizing — dolomite kicking in or hard water buildup |

### Salt Creep & Flushing

**Salt creep:** synthetic-feed soil accumulates salts over weeks. EC drift in runoff is the early warning. Once runoff EC exceeds input by ~1.0+, plant uptake suffers (osmotic resistance).

**Flushing** (in soil, conservative use):
- **When:** runoff EC very high; nute burn signs; pre-harvest debate (controversial — see `12-flowering.md` flush debate)
- **How:** plain pH'd water, **1.5× pot volume**, in a slow saturate-and-drain cycle. Don't soak the pot multiple times — you risk anaerobic root damage.
- **Don't routinely flush** — soil reset is rarely the right answer; correct the input EC instead.

## Feeding Schedule (Synthetic in Soil)

### Why Amended Soil Self-Feeds First

The amendments in pre-made hot mixes break down through microbial activity and release nutrients over weeks. **Adding synthetic feed during the self-feeding window stacks nutrients on top of nutrients = lockout / burn.**

### When to Start Feeding

Watch for these signals:
- First mature fan leaves at the bottom of the plant just starting to lighten (mild N draw)
- Plant has clearly outgrown its soil's runway (~week 3–4 in Ocean Forest, ~week 1–2 in Roots Original, day 1 in Pro-Mix HP)
- Runoff EC drops to 0.4–0.6 (substrate is thirsty for nutrients)

### EC Progression

In soil with no major amendments (Pro-Mix HP equivalent):

| Stage | Input EC (synthetic feed) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Seedling | 0.4 – 0.6 | Quarter to half-strength |
| Early veg | 0.8 – 1.2 | Ramp up |
| Mid veg | 1.2 – 1.6 | Full veg strength |
| Flower stretch | 1.4 – 1.8 | Transition to bloom |
| Mid flower | 1.6 – 2.0 | Peak demand |
| Late flower | 1.4 – 1.8 | Taper |
| Last week | 0 – 0.4 | Plain water or final flush per preference |

In hot mixes (Ocean Forest, super soil): start at 0% feed, add only when plant signals demand. Top-dress dry amendments late in flower if needed (see `06-nutrients-organic.md`).

### Feed-Water-Feed-Water vs Every-Watering

In synthetic soil:
- **Feed every watering at lower EC** (1.0–1.4 for veg, 1.4–1.8 for flower) — modern preferred approach in soil-less mixes; mimics coco fertigation logic
- **Feed alternate waterings at higher EC** (1.5–2.0 veg, 2.0–2.5 flower, plain water in between) — old-school approach; works but less precise

In hot soil: water-only most of cycle; feed only when signals demand.

## Soil-Specific Failure Modes

### Compaction
Repeated watering pressure compresses soil over time → reduced oxygen → root health declines. Especially in plastic pots after 6+ weeks.
- **Prevention:** higher % perlite (25%+), fabric pots (less compaction), gentle hand-watering
- **Fix late-stage:** poke holes with chopstick to re-aerate; up-pot if early enough

### Hydrophobia (Bone-Dry Soil Rejecting Water)
Severely dried-out peat-based soil becomes hydrophobic — water beads off, runs down sides, doesn't penetrate.
- **Prevention:** never let soil go fully bone-dry
- **Fix:** soak the pot in a tray of warm water (1 hr) until weight returns; or apply a wetting agent (Yucca extract, Coco-Wet) to break surface tension; resume normal cycle

### Anaerobic / "Sour" Soil
Chronic overwatering → no oxygen in root zone → anaerobic bacteria dominate → smelly, dark, rotten soil + root death.
- **Prevention:** wet/dry discipline, drainage holes, fabric pots
- **Fix:** stop watering, let dry to almost-bone-dry (push the limits this once), then resume conservative cycle. Severe cases may require up-pot to fresh dry mix.

### Fungus Gnats
Always-wet top inch of soil = fungus gnat paradise. See `08-pests.md` for full IPM.
- **Quick fix:** let top dry out fully between waterings; consider a perlite or sand top-dress (1/2 inch); BTI mosquito-bit drench

### Salt Buildup
Synthetic-fed soil over many weeks accumulates salts → EC creep → osmotic uptake resistance.
- **Prevention:** 10–20% runoff every watering; periodic plain-water session
- **Fix:** leach with 1.5× pot volume plain pH'd water, slow saturate-and-drain

### Root-Bound
Roots have filled the pot and started circling (plastic) or crowding the wall (fabric). Limits new root expansion.
- **Tell:** watering dries down very fast (under 24 hr in expected cycle); runoff comes through fast (no soil retention)
- **Fix:** up-pot

## Confusion / Misdiagnosis Traps

| Looks like | Actually | How to tell |
|---|---|---|
| Underwatering (drooping leaves) | Overwatering — both wilt | Pot weight: light = under, heavy = over. Plus underwater plants perk up after watering; overwatered ones don't |
| Nutrient deficiency in week 2 | Feeding too early on hot mix | Skip the feed, water only, watch for recovery |
| Nutrient deficiency in week 6 with no feeding | Hot mix has run out of runway | Start a moderate feed, watch for response |
| pH lockout | Salt buildup with normal pH | Runoff EC significantly above input → leach |
| "Soil pH is 6.5 in runoff so I'm fine" | Runoff lags substrate by hours; substrate could be drifting | Slurry test for true substrate pH if symptoms appear |
| Calcium deficiency in soil | Dolomite hasn't kicked in OR low transpiration / high RH | Verify VPD first per `02-environment.md`; Cal-Mg supplement if env is good |
| Runoff EC is 0 in week 4 | Plant has used up the salts; feeding window opened | Begin the feed schedule |
| "My pH meter says 6.3 but plants look pH lockout" | pH meter not calibrated; calibration solution old | Recalibrate meter monthly; replace solution every 6 months |
| Yellowing in late flower in soil | Normal "fade" / N drawdown | Don't push N this late in flower |
| Brown wet bottom of pot when lifting | Anaerobic root rot zone | Stop watering, let dry; up-pot if severe |
| Mushrooms growing in soil | Healthy biology in organic-amended mix | Cosmetic only; no harm |

## Quick Reference

### Mix Selection by Use Case

| Use case | Mix |
|---|---|
| Seedling / clone | Light mix (Pro-Mix HP, Roots Original) |
| Production veg → flower with synthetic feeds | Light or medium (Pro-Mix HP, Happy Frog) |
| Want minimal feeding | Hot mix (Ocean Forest) or super soil layered |
| Veganic / organic | Medium-amended (Roots Original) + organic top-dress |
| No-till / once-and-done | BAS or KIS Coots — see `04-medium-living-soil.md` |

### Pot Size by Plant Size (Indoor)

| Plant final height | Pot size |
|---|---|
| 12–18 inches (autos, micro grows) | 1–3 gal |
| 24–36 inches (most indoor) | 3–5 gal |
| 36–48 inches | 5–7 gal |
| 48+ inches (long veg, mother plants) | 7–15 gal |

### Watering Cycle (5-gal fabric, indoor, mid-cycle)

| Plant stage | Frequency | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Seedling | Every 3–5 days | 50–100 ml |
| Early veg | Every 3–4 days | 0.5–1 gal |
| Mature veg | Every 2–3 days | 1.0–1.5 gal |
| Mid flower | Every 1–2 days | 1.0–1.6 gal |
| Late flower | Daily | 1.0–1.5 gal |

### pH/EC Card

| Reading | Soil sweet spot | Hard limit |
|---|---|---|
| Input pH | 6.3 – 6.8 | 6.0 – 7.0 |
| Substrate pH (slurry) | 6.3 – 6.8 | 6.0 – 7.0 |
| Runoff EC vs input EC | Within ±0.5 | More than 1.0 above input = leach |
| Input EC veg (synthetic) | 1.0 – 1.6 | — |
| Input EC flower (synthetic) | 1.6 – 2.0 | — |
