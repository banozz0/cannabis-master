# 07 — Nutrient Deficiencies & Toxicities

Complete reference for diagnosing nutrient issues by visual symptom, root cause, and corrective action. Cross-reference with `04-medium-*.md` (uptake mechanics differ by medium), `05-water-quality.md` (pH and water profile), and `06-nutrients-*.md` (feed schedules).

## How to Use This File

1. **Identify mobile vs immobile element first** — narrows search 50%
2. **Check pH BEFORE diagnosing** — most "deficiencies" are lockouts, not actual shortage
3. **Match visual pattern to element** — use the diagnostic tree below
4. **Verify with substrate/runoff data** before treating
5. **Treat root cause, not symptom** — adding the missing element rarely works if pH is the issue

## Critical Concept — Mobile vs Immobile

Plants move some elements around when supply runs short, others stay put. This determines where symptoms appear first.

| Mobility | Elements | Symptoms appear on |
|---|---|---|
| **Mobile** | N, P, K, Mg, Zn (partially) | OLD/lower leaves first — plant moves it up to new growth |
| **Immobile** | Ca, S, Fe, Mn, B, Cu, Mo, Si | NEW/upper leaves first — can't be moved from old leaves |

**Diagnostic shortcut:** Yellowing or damage starting at the bottom of the plant = mobile element issue. Starting at the top = immobile element issue.

## pH Sweet Spots by Medium (memorize these)

| Medium | pH Range | Optimal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Soil | 6.0–7.0 | 6.3–6.8 | Buffers itself somewhat |
| Coco | 5.5–6.3 | 5.8–6.0 | Hydroponic, no buffer |
| DWC/Hydro | 5.5–6.3 | 5.8–6.0 | Drift up over time as plant feeds |
| Living soil | 6.0–7.0 | 6.3–6.8 | Don't pH adjust water — biology handles it |

**Outside these ranges = lockout, not deficiency.** Adding more of the "missing" nutrient won't help if roots can't absorb it.

## pH Lockout Quick Map

What gets locked out at extreme pH:

| pH range | Locked out |
|---|---|
| Below 5.5 (acidic) | Ca, Mg, P, K, Mo |
| 5.5–6.5 (sweet spot most mediums) | Nothing significantly |
| Above 6.5 in coco/hydro | Fe, Mn, B, Cu, Zn (micros) |
| Above 7.0 in soil | P, Fe, Mn, Zn |

If user reports a micronutrient deficiency in coco/hydro → check pH FIRST. 90% of "iron deficiencies" in hydro are pH drift above 6.3.

## MACRONUTRIENTS

### Nitrogen (N) — Mobile

**Role:** Vegetative growth, chlorophyll, protein synthesis, leaf mass.

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Lower/older leaves yellow uniformly (not interveinal)
- Yellowing starts at leaf tip, moves inward
- Eventual leaf drop from bottom up
- Stunted growth, pale green overall
- Sometimes purple/red stems (mild)

**Stage-specific normal vs problem:**
- Late flower (week 6+): some lower-leaf yellowing is NORMAL ripening, not deficiency. Don't feed more N this late.
- Veg/early flower: yellowing = real deficiency, fix it.

**Toxicity symptoms:**
- Dark green, glossy "claw" — leaf tips curve down
- Dark green leaves overall, soft and brittle
- Slow flowering / delayed bloom
- Excessive vegetative growth in flower
- Burnt leaf tips (combined with too much salt)

**Root causes (deficiency):**
1. Underfeeding — most common in soil after week 4-5 if not amended
2. pH lockout (soil >7.5)
3. Cold root zone (<15°C) — uptake slows
4. Light feeders fed too lightly
5. Old soil depleted

**Root causes (toxicity):**
1. Overfeeding, especially synthetic in flower
2. Veg nutes used into flower
3. High N-week numbers in mfg schedule for the strain
4. Manure/blood meal heavy organic mix in flower

**Fix (deficiency):**
- Feed at scheduled EC, check pH at 6.3 (soil) or 5.8 (coco/hydro)
- If pH off → flush + reset to correct range
- Veg nutes: increase EC by 0.2 next feed, monitor 3 days
- Organic: top-dress with light blood meal or worm castings

**Fix (toxicity):**
- Stop N feeds immediately
- In coco/hydro: flush with plain pH'd water at 1.5x pot volume, resume at lower EC
- In soil: water plain pH'd water 2-3 cycles, then resume at 70% strength
- Wait 5-7 days for new growth to show recovery

**Confidence checks:**
- Confirm: lower leaves only? → N
- Rule out: if upper new growth also pale → could be S or Fe (immobile), not N
- Rule out: interveinal pattern? → likely Mg (mobile too) not N

---

### Phosphorus (P) — Mobile

**Role:** Root development, energy transfer (ATP), flowering, bud formation.

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Lower leaves: dark green or blue-green (early stage)
- Progresses to purple/red on stems and leaf undersides
- Brown/bronze necrotic spots on older leaves
- Leaves curl downward, become brittle
- Stunted growth, especially in flower
- Reduced bud size

**Don't confuse with:**
- Genetics: many strains naturally show purple stems — not deficiency
- Cold: temps under 15°C cause purpling without P shortage
- Anthocyanins from cold nights / high light = cosmetic, not P issue

**Toxicity symptoms:**
- Rare in cannabis — usually shows as Zn, Cu, Fe lockout (P interferes with micro uptake)
- Leaf-tip burn similar to N tox

**Root causes (deficiency):**
1. pH lockout (soil <6.0 OR >7.0; coco/hydro <5.5 OR >6.5)
2. Cold root zone — P uptake plummets below 15°C
3. Underfeeding in flower transition (P demand spikes weeks 1-3 of flower)
4. Old / depleted organic soil

**Fix:**
- Verify pH first
- Warm root zone if cold (heating mat under hydro res, insulate floor)
- Increase bloom feed by 0.2 EC
- Foliar with diluted PK supplement (1/4 strength) for emergency

---

### Potassium (K) — Mobile

**Role:** Water regulation, stomata, terpene/oil production, disease resistance, bud density.

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- Leaf TIP burn — yellowing/browning starting at the very tip
- Tips curl upward (taco) before browning
- Margins (edges) yellow then necrotic, working inward
- Pattern from outside-in (opposite of Mg which is inside-out)
- Lower leaves first (mobile)
- Spotting between veins on older leaves
- Stretched / weak stems
- Reduced flower density and weight

**Don't confuse with:**
- Salt buildup / over-fertilization — also burns tips. Check EC.
- Light burn — top leaves only
- Heat stress — taco shape but no necrosis pattern

**Toxicity symptoms:**
- Locks out Mg, Mn, Zn — shows as those deficiencies
- Salt accumulation, EC creep in substrate

**Root causes:**
1. Underfeeding K specifically (often happens with DIY feeds)
2. Heavy N feeding suppresses K uptake (N:K ratio off)
3. pH lockout (soil <6.0)
4. Calcium excess locks out K
5. Old depleted soil

**Fix:**
- pH check first
- Verify EC — if too low, raise; if too high (salt-up), flush
- Increase bloom-stage K (PK boost product or higher-K base)
- Don't over-correct — K toxicity is worse than mild deficiency

---

### Calcium (Ca) — Immobile

**Role:** Cell wall structure, root tip health, signal transduction, prevents disease.

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- NEW growth distortion — upper leaves wrinkled, twisted, hooked
- Brown necrotic spots scattered on younger leaves (not just margin)
- Leaf tips dying / "burnt" appearance on new growth
- Weak stems, snap easily
- Root tips brown, slow root development
- Hollow/brown stems internally
- "Tip burn" on new shoots — different from K (which is older leaves)

**Specific to medium:**
- Coco: VERY common because coco binds Ca. Mandatory Cal-Mg supplement.
- DWC/hydro: common with RO water (no native Ca)
- Soil: rare unless pH crashed below 5.5

**Toxicity:**
- Locks out K, Mg, Fe
- Rarely from feeding — usually from pH-up overuse or hard tap water + nutes

**Root causes:**
1. RO water without Cal-Mg (#1 cause)
2. Coco substrate without Cal-Mg supplement
3. pH below 5.5 (acidic lockout)
4. Low transpiration (high RH, no airflow → Ca doesn't move via transpiration stream)
5. Inconsistent watering — Ca needs steady water flow

**Fix:**
- Add Cal-Mg supplement at mfg dose if not already
- Increase airflow + lower RH if too humid (>65% in flower)
- Verify pH at 5.8-6.0 (coco/hydro) or 6.3-6.8 (soil)
- Foliar Cal-Mg at 1ml/L as emergency

---

### Magnesium (Mg) — Mobile

**Role:** Chlorophyll core (literal magnesium atom in chlorophyll molecule), enzyme activator, photosynthesis.

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- INTERVEINAL yellowing — green veins, yellow tissue between
- Lower/older leaves first (mobile)
- Pattern moves UP the plant if untreated
- Eventually rust/red-brown spots appear in yellow zones
- Leaves brittle, can curl up at edges
- Distinctive "Christmas tree" pattern in mid-flower if chronic

**Confusion risk:**
- Iron deficiency = same interveinal pattern but on NEW growth (Fe is immobile)
- If interveinal is on lower leaves = Mg. Upper = Fe.

**Toxicity:**
- Rare — usually fine to over-supplement modestly
- Excess locks out Ca, K

**Root causes:**
1. RO water (no native Mg) without Cal-Mg
2. Coco without Cal-Mg
3. pH below 5.0 or above 7.5 (lockout)
4. K excess locks out Mg
5. Cold root zone

**Fix:**
- Cal-Mg supplement at mfg dose (Epsom salts as cheap alternative: 1g/L = 1 tsp/gal)
- Check pH 5.8 (coco/hydro) / 6.3-6.8 (soil)
- Reduce K if over-fed
- Foliar Epsom (5g/L) if severe — fast green-up in 24-48h

---

### Sulfur (S) — Immobile

**Role:** Amino acids, proteins, terpene/sulfur compounds (cannabis aroma), enzymes.

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- New/upper leaves uniformly yellow (looks like N def but on top, not bottom)
- Whole-leaf yellowing, no interveinal pattern
- Veins yellow ALONG with leaf tissue (vs Fe/Mg where veins stay green)
- Stunted growth, slow
- Sometimes purple stems
- Reduced terpene production

**Confusion risk:**
- N deficiency = lower leaves. S = upper.
- Fe deficiency = upper leaves but interveinal pattern. S = uniform yellow.

**Toxicity:**
- Rare in cannabis
- Can lower pH (sulfuric acid type pH-down adds S)

**Root causes:**
1. Underfeeding — rare in commercial nute lines
2. RO water + custom feed without S source
3. Cold root zone

**Fix:**
- Switch to nute line that includes S (most commercial blends do)
- Epsom salts add S along with Mg (1g/L)
- Check pH

## SECONDARY MACROS / MICROS

### Iron (Fe) — Immobile

**Role:** Chlorophyll synthesis (not part of molecule but required to make it), electron transport.

**Deficiency symptoms:**
- NEW/upper leaves: bright yellow with sharp dark green veins
- Interveinal but very pronounced — veins look "drawn on"
- Whole new growth pale/white-yellow if severe
- Eventually necrotic spots in yellow tissue
- Plant shows fastest visual response to fix (24-48h green-up)

**Toxicity:**
- Bronzing of leaves
- Rare from overfeeding — usually pH/lockout reversal

**Root causes:**
1. **pH above 6.3 in coco/hydro = #1 cause** (confirm this first ALWAYS)
2. Above 7.0 in soil
3. Cold root zone
4. P excess locks out Fe
5. RO water without micros + custom feed

**Fix:**
- pH adjust to 5.8 (coco/hydro) or 6.3-6.8 (soil) IMMEDIATELY
- If pH correct: chelated iron supplement (Fe-EDDHA works at higher pH, Fe-DTPA mid, Fe-EDTA low pH only)
- Foliar chelated Fe for fast response

---

### Manganese (Mn) — Immobile

**Symptoms:** Interveinal yellow on upper leaves WITH brown/grey necrotic flecks throughout. Looks like Fe but with spotting. Veins stay green but smaller veins also pale.

**Causes:** pH above 6.3 (lockout), Fe excess.

**Fix:** pH first. Then mfg micro supplement.

---

### Zinc (Zn) — Partially mobile

**Symptoms:** New growth — short internodes, "rosette" of small leaves clustered. Interveinal yellowing with leaf cupping. Twisted, distorted leaves.

**Causes:** pH above 6.5 (very sensitive), high P locks Zn.

**Fix:** pH adjust, lower bloom P if overfed, Zn supplement.

---

### Copper (Cu) — Immobile

**Symptoms:** Upper leaves dark/blue-green with bronze/silver sheen, leaf tips wilted, twisted growth, rare deficiency.

**Causes:** Very alkaline pH, organic-heavy soil binding Cu.

**Fix:** pH adjust, Cu supplement at trace amounts only.

---

### Boron (B) — Immobile

**Symptoms:** Brown/dead growing tips ("burnt"), thick leaves, hollow stems, abnormal new growth, brittle.

**Causes:** Low transpiration (humidity too high), pH lockout, B is rarely supplemented separately.

**Toxicity:** Easy to overdose — leaf tip yellowing then necrosis. Less is more.

**Fix:** Improve airflow + lower RH. Cal-Mg products often contain B.

---

### Molybdenum (Mo) — Immobile

**Symptoms:** Yellow lower leaves with twisted growth, rare. Often mistaken for N def.

**Causes:** pH below 5.5 (acidic lockout). Almost never actual shortage.

**Fix:** Raise pH to 5.8+. Mfg nute lines have enough Mo.

---

### Silicon (Si) — Beneficial, not technically essential

**Role:** Cell wall strength, pest/disease resistance, heat tolerance, stronger stems.

**Adding Si is recommended but not "deficiency-driven."** Use a Si supplement (Mono-Si or potassium silicate) at 0.5-1ml/L through veg and early flower. Add to water FIRST before other nutes (raises pH).

## DIAGNOSTIC TREE (Quick Reference)

```
START → Where on the plant?

LOWER/OLDER LEAVES affected first?
├── Uniform yellow leaf → N deficiency
├── Yellow with purple stems / dark green pre-yellow → P deficiency
├── Tip burn moving inward + margin yellow → K deficiency
├── Interveinal yellow, green veins → Mg deficiency
└── Spots, blotches, irregular → likely disease, see 09-disease.md

UPPER/NEW LEAVES affected first?
├── Uniform yellow → S deficiency
├── Interveinal yellow, sharp green veins → Fe deficiency
├── Interveinal yellow with brown spots → Mn deficiency
├── Distorted/twisted/wrinkled → Ca deficiency (also check B)
├── Short internodes, rosette pattern → Zn deficiency
├── Dark blue-green with bronze sheen → Cu deficiency
└── Dead growing tips → B deficiency

WHOLE PLANT affected at once?
├── Drooping no color change → watering issue, see 04-medium-*.md
├── All leaves dark green clawing → N toxicity
├── Tip burn everywhere → salt buildup / EC too high
└── Sudden wilting all stages → root rot or major pH crash

HEAT/LIGHT-LOOKING but might be nutrient?
├── Top leaves taco/curl up → could be heat OR K (check temp first)
├── Leaves bleach white at top → light burn, raise fixture
└── Foxtailing in late flower → genetics + heat, not nute

ALWAYS CHECK FIRST:
1. pH of runoff (or res for hydro)
2. EC of runoff (or res)
3. Substrate moisture (over/underwater?)
4. Recent feed change?
5. Recent environment change?
```

## Common Misdiagnosis Traps

| Looks like | Actually | How to tell |
|---|---|---|
| N deficiency in late flower | Normal ripening | Week 7+ flower, lower fade is desired |
| Fe deficiency | pH lockout | Check pH first, 90% of "Fe def" is pH drift |
| Mg deficiency | Cal-Mg shortage from RO water | Always pair Cal+Mg in coco/hydro |
| K deficiency tip burn | Light burn on top leaves | K = lower leaves, light burn = top only |
| Multiple deficiencies at once | Single pH lockout cascading | Reset pH, watch single recovery |
| Ca deficiency | Low transpiration | Improve airflow before adding more Ca |
| P deficiency purple | Cold-induced anthocyanin | Check root zone temp, often genetic |
| Cu deficiency | Septoria fungal disease | Cu = whole leaf color, septoria = spots |

## Verification Questions Before Treating

Before recommending any fix:
1. What's the runoff/res pH?
2. What's the runoff/res EC?
3. What stage is the plant in?
4. When was the last feed and at what EC?
5. What's the room temp and RH at the time?
6. Has the user changed anything in the last 5-7 days?
7. Is this on multiple plants or just one? (One plant = likely genetics, multiple = environmental)

If 3+ of these are unknown → don't diagnose yet. Get the data.
