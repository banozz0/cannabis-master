# 12 — Flowering

Flowering is the productive payoff of all upstream work — environment, nutrition, genetics, training. It's also the highest-stakes stage because there is no recovery before harvest. Mistakes in late flower compound (a missed RH adjustment in week 6 = Botrytis in week 8 = lost crop). This file covers the complete flowering biology and the stage-by-stage progression: pre-flower → stretch → mid-flower → late flower → ripening, with photoperiod and autoflower models treated separately. Cross-reference with `03-lighting.md` (DLI/PPFD targets shift through flower; far-red EOD; stretch implications), `02-environment.md` (RH must drop progressively to prevent late-flower disease), `06-nutrients-synthetic.md` and `06-nutrients-organic.md` (PK shift, taper math, flush debate), `07-deficiencies.md` (late-flower senescence vs actual deficiency — the #1 misdiagnosis at week 7+), `09-disease.md` (Botrytis is the late-flower nightmare; environment is the prevention), `10-training.md` (training stops here; SCROG tucks until ~day 10 of flower), `11-propagation.md` (mother plants stay vegetative; pre-flower sex check on production plants), `13-harvest.md` (this file's ripening section hands off to harvest decision).

## How to Use This File

1. **Time the flip correctly** — plant size + training complete + space + recovery from last HST cut.
2. **Run the stretch protocol** — anticipate 1.5–3× height multiplier; raise lights weekly; finish all SCROG tucking by day 10.
3. **Execute mid-flower bulking** — peak EC + PK shift + week-3 schwazz/lollipop window (cross-ref `10-training.md`).
4. **Drop RH progressively for late flower** — 50–60% stretch → 40–50% late → 40–45% ripening; this single lever prevents most late-flower disease.
5. **Read trichomes daily from week 8** — clear → cloudy → amber; harvest window depends on desired effect profile.
6. **Don't fight senescence** — late-flower lower-leaf yellowing is normal nitrogen redistribution, not a deficiency.
7. **Hand off to `13-harvest.md`** when trichome ratio matches target.

## Critical Concept — Photoperiod Biology

Cannabis is a short-day plant — flowering is triggered by uninterrupted dark periods exceeding a critical threshold. The mechanism is the **phytochrome system**, the same red/far-red light sensor used by every higher plant.

**The phytochrome cycle (Tier 1 — multiple peer-reviewed; Bugbee Utah State CPL; Apicella et al. 2022 *Plant Direct*):**
- **PhyA** and **PhyB** are the two main phytochrome forms in cannabis
- **Pr (inactive form)** absorbs **red light (~660 nm)** → converts to Pfr
- **Pfr (active form)** absorbs **far-red light (~730 nm)** → converts back to Pr
- Pfr also reverts to Pr slowly over time in darkness (the "dark reversion")
- During light cycle: Pfr accumulates (red dominates conversion)
- During dark cycle: Pfr decays back to Pr over hours

**The flowering signal cascade:**
1. Dark period exceeds critical length (~12 hours for most cultivars; ~13 hours for some equatorial sativas)
2. Pfr drops below threshold concentration in leaves
3. **Florigen** (a mobile flowering signal) is produced in leaves
4. Florigen travels through vascular system to shoot apical meristems
5. Meristems switch from vegetative growth to reproductive structures (calyxes, pistils, trichomes)

**Why dark interruption matters:**
- A brief light interruption during dark cycle (even seconds of red-spectrum light) re-converts Pr → Pfr
- The Pfr threshold for florigen production is reset
- Dark period effectively "restarts"
- Result: stalled or reversed flowering, hermaphroditism risk, stress response

**Recent research (Tier 1 — PMC 2024 *Longer Photoperiod Substantially Increases Indoor-Grown Cannabis' Yield and Quality*):** some cultivars yield more under **13/11** than 12/12, suggesting the critical photoperiod for many modern hybrids is closer to 13 hours of light maximum. This is an emerging area — most growers still default to 12/12, but 13/11 is being trialed in commercial.

**Autoflowers don't use this system** — they carry *Cannabis ruderalis* day-neutral genetics; flowering is age-triggered, not photoperiod-triggered.

## Critical Concept — Cycle Stages Map

```
PRE-FLIP (last week veg) → THE FLIP →
WEEK 1–3 STRETCH (1.5–3× height) →
WEEK 2–3 BUD-SET (calyxes form, stretch ends) →
WEEK 3–5 MID-FLOWER (bulking, peak EC) →
WEEK 6–7 LATE FLOWER (density, fade begins) →
WEEK 8+ RIPENING (trichome maturation) →
HARVEST WINDOW (cross to 13-harvest.md)
```

Each stage has its own biology, environment, and feed targets. They're not interchangeable — running mid-flower environment in late flower invites Botrytis; running ripening feed in mid-flower starves the bulking phase.

## Critical Concept — Stretch Math

The stretch period (early flower) doubles to triples plant height as gibberellins drive stem elongation in preparation for reproductive structures. Plan ceiling clearance accordingly.

| Genetic dominance | Stretch multiplier | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Pure indica | 1.3–1.7× pre-flip height | Flip later (75% screen full); minimal ceiling concern |
| Indica-leaning hybrid | 1.5–2× | Standard flip timing |
| Balanced hybrid | 2–2.5× | Earlier flip; SCROG with vertical room |
| Sativa-leaning hybrid | 2.5–3× | Flip much earlier (50–60% screen full); plan tall space |
| Pure sativa | 3–4× (sometimes more) | Earliest flip; aggressive LST in stretch; tall ceilings required |

(Tier 2 — Royal Queen Seeds, Dutch Passion strain documentation; Tier 3 — community consensus across cultivar databases.)

**Stretch duration:** typically 1–3 weeks; peaks week 2; complete by end of week 3 in most cultivars.

**Practical math:** 4-foot tent + 2-foot pre-flip plant + 3× stretch = 6-foot canopy hitting the 4-foot ceiling. Always calculate before flip.

## Critical Concept — Trichome Maturation Timeline

The harvest decision rests on trichome inspection. The visual progression is:

| Stage | Appearance | Cannabinoid status | Effect profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1 — Clear / glassy** | Translucent, like glass beads | Premature; THC not fully synthesized | Avoid harvest; insufficient potency |
| **Stage 2 — Cloudy / milky** | Opaque, white-ish, "milky" | Peak THC content | Energetic, cerebral, "sativa" effect |
| **Stage 3 — Mixed (~70% cloudy / 30% amber)** | Mostly milky with growing amber percentage | Balanced THC + degradation products (CBN beginning) | Balanced effect; standard mid-window harvest |
| **Stage 4 — Mostly amber** | Yellow-orange dominant | THCA oxidizing → CBN ("sleepy" cannabinoid) | Sedating, "couch-lock," indica preference |

**Inspection technique:**
- Jeweler's loupe (30–60×) for quick canopy checks
- USB microscope (60–200×) for precise trichome reading
- Examine **bud calyx trichomes**, NOT sugar leaf — calyx mature first; sugar leaves lag
- Multiple bud sites — top, middle, lower — they don't all ripen simultaneously
- Daily inspection from week 8

**Standard balanced harvest window:** 80–90% cloudy with 10–20% amber. Slightly earlier (50–70% cloudy, no amber) for sativa-leaning effect; later (50% amber+) for sedating effect.

(Tier 1 — Caplan & Zheng Guelph harvest timing research; Tier 3 — Ed Rosenthal *Judging Cannabis Ripeness by Trichomes* primary source.)

**Critical: amber is THCA degrading to CBN** — this is documented chemistry, not folk wisdom. THC peaks at cloudy; waiting "longer" past cloudy doesn't increase THC, it converts it to CBN.

## Critical Concept — Cannabinoid Biosynthesis

(Tier 1 — *The biosynthesis of the cannabinoids* PMC 2021; Apicella et al. 2022 *Plant Direct* — gene regulation during flower maturation.)

Cannabinoids are produced enzymatically in **glandular trichomes** during flowering, from a single precursor that branches into THCA, CBDA, and CBCA via three different synthase enzymes.

**The pathway:**
1. **Hexanoic acid + malonyl-CoA → olivetolic acid** (via olivetolic acid cyclase)
2. **Olivetolic acid + GPP → CBGA (cannabigerolic acid)** — the "mother cannabinoid" (via aromatic prenyltransferase)
3. **CBGA branches three ways** via stereoselective cyclization:
   - **THCAS (THCA synthase)** → THCA (tetrahydrocannabinolic acid)
   - **CBDAS (CBDA synthase)** → CBDA (cannabidiolic acid)
   - **CBCAS (CBCA synthase)** → CBCA (cannabichromenic acid)
4. **Decarboxylation** (heat / time / light) → THC, CBD, CBC (the active neutral forms)

**Key facts:**
- Living plants produce ACID forms (THCA, not THC); decarboxylation happens at harvest, drying, or smoking
- THCAS and CBDAS are competing enzymes — strain genetics determine which dominates (Type I = THC-dominant; Type II = balanced; Type III = CBD-dominant)
- Cannabinoid biosynthetic genes are **upregulated during flower maturation** — production peaks during late flower / early ripening
- Trichome glandular heads are the production sites; bigger / denser trichomes = more biosynthesis capacity

**Why this matters operationally:**
- Flower stage is when biosynthesis genes are most active — environmental stress here directly reduces cannabinoid output
- Late-flower feed strategy should NOT starve the plant of S (sulfur — terpene precursor) or P (phosphorus — energy currency for biosynthesis)
- Cool nights in late flower (65–72°F) preserve trichomes and slow degradation

## THE FLIP — Flip Timing and Dark-Cycle Sanctity

**When to flip (photoperiod plants):**
- Training complete (mainline cuts done 7–10+ days ago)
- Plant size accommodates expected stretch (calculate ceiling clearance)
- SCROG screen 50–75% full (depending on stretch class)
- Mother room timing aligned with clone production
- Healthy plant — not stressed from recent issue

**Standard schedule: 12/12** (12 hours light, 12 hours uninterrupted dark)

**Schedule variants:**
| Schedule | Use case |
|---|---|
| **12/12** | Standard; works for all photoperiod cultivars |
| **13/11** | Recent research (Tier 1 — PMC 2024) suggests yield gains in some cultivars; experimental |
| **11/13** | Slight extra dark used by some commercial for color/density |
| **10/14** | Autumn-mimicking; outdoor-replication indoor |
| **12/12 from seed** | Rare; small fast plants; skips veg entirely |

**Light-leak rules (cross-ref `03-lighting.md`):**
- ZERO light during dark cycle — even LED indicator lights matter
- Tape over electronic lights
- Light traps on tent vents
- Verify by sitting in dark room 10 min, then look for any visible source
- Distant streetlight through window crack? Block it

**Hermaphroditism risk** (Tier 2 — multiple sources; biology cross-ref `01-genetics-strains.md`): light interruption during dark cycle decreases ethylene (female-promoting hormone), increases gibberellins (male-promoting); plant produces "rescue" male flowers attempting self-pollination. One light leak event can hermie an entire room of stress-prone genetics.

**Dark-cycle sanctity is THE most important environmental rule of flowering. Get this wrong and nothing else matters.**

## PRE-FLOWER (Sex Determination)

For photoperiod plants from seed (clones already inherit mother's sex):

**When to expect pre-flowers:** 4–6 weeks after sprout, regardless of light schedule (the plant is signaling its readiness to reproduce when the day comes).

**Sex identification (at internode between branch and main stem):**

| Sex | Pre-flower appearance |
|---|---|
| **Female** | 2 white pistils (hair-like fibers) emerging from a small calyx — keep these |
| **Male** | Round "balls" / pollen sacs, no pistils — remove immediately to prevent pollination |
| **Hermaphrodite** | Both pistils AND balls on same plant — usually due to stress; cull unless breeding |

**Removal of males:** as soon as identified, before pollen sacs open. If a male flowers in a sinsemilla room, every female within range is at risk of pollination → seedy flower (reduced quality + market value).

**Sexing for clones:** N/A — clones inherit mother's sex; PCR-based sex tests exist for very early sexing (TumiGenomics offers cannabis sex test).

**Importance for breeding** (cross-ref `18-breeding.md`): pre-flower observation is when intentional breeders identify desirable males to keep for pollen; sinsemilla growers cull all males.

## STRETCH (Week 1–3 Flower) — Per-Stage Deep Profile

**Visible signs:**
- Rapid vertical growth (visible day-to-day, sometimes hour-to-hour)
- Inter-node elongation (stems grow longer between leaf pairs)
- New calyxes forming at growing tips
- Plant generally taller and more open

**Internal biology:**
- Gibberellin shift drives stem elongation
- Hormone reorganization for reproductive growth
- Phytochrome signal (florigen) reaching apical meristems
- Beginning of cannabinoid gene upregulation

**Environmental targets:**
- RH: 50–60%
- Temp: 75–78°F day, 65–72°F night
- VPD: 1.0–1.3 kPa
- PPFD: 700–900 µmol·m⁻²·s⁻¹ (raise lights weekly to stay above canopy)
- Photoperiod: strict 12/12 (or 13/11 if experimenting)

**Feed targets (cross-ref `06-nutrients-synthetic.md` and `06-nutrients-organic.md`):**
- Continue veg-style feed first week of flower
- Begin PK shift week 2
- EC target: 1.6–2.0 (synthetic, standard PPFD)
- pH per medium

**Training allowed:**
- LST tucking under SCROG screen
- Final SCROG weave (continue until day ~10)
- Light selective fan removal
- **NO HST** — anything cut here doesn't recover before bud-set

**Common issues:**
- Stretch outpaces ceiling (stretch math underestimated → light burn risk)
- Over-watering during fast growth (plant uptake increases dramatically)
- Hermaphrodite tendencies revealed (stress-prone strains under any light leak)
- Light bleach at top canopy (light too close to fast-growing plants)

## MID-FLOWER (Week 3–5 Bulking) — Per-Stage Deep Profile

**Visible signs:**
- Stretch ending; vertical growth slows or stops
- Bud sites set; calyxes swelling visibly
- Pistils prolific and white
- Trichomes beginning to appear on calyxes
- Plant directing energy to reproductive structures

**Internal biology:**
- Peak photosynthesis demand (highest of any stage)
- Cannabinoid biosynthesis ramping (gene upregulation peaks week 4–5)
- Trichome production accelerating
- Terpene precursor synthesis

**Environmental targets:**
- RH: 45–55%
- Temp: 72–78°F day, 65–72°F night
- VPD: 1.2–1.5 kPa
- PPFD: 800–1000+ µmol·m⁻²·s⁻¹ (+ CO2 if available pushes upward)
- CO2 supplementation valuable here (1000–1500 ppm) — peak photosynthesis demand

**Feed targets:**
- **Peak EC**: 2.0–2.6 (synthetic, standard PPFD; higher with CO2)
- **PK shift** in full effect — bloom-stage feed
- pH stable at medium target
- Cal-Mg sufficient for high-demand bulking

**Training allowed:**
- **Week 3 schwazz #2 + lollipop window** (cross-ref `10-training.md` — final training event)
- Light fan-leaf removal blocking direct bud sites
- **NO HST after week 3** — buds developing; cuts compromise yield

**Common issues:**
- Heat stress at peak demand (turn down dimmer, raise light, increase exhaust)
- PK over-boost lockout cascade (too much P→Zn/Fe lockout, see `07-deficiencies.md`)
- Bud rot first signs (cross-ref `09-disease.md`) — usually from environmental drift
- Foxtailing onset (heat stress + LED too close)

---

## LATE FLOWER (Week 6–7 Density) — Per-Stage Deep Profile

**Visible signs:**
- Bud density increasing (calyxes swelling; bud weight increasing visibly)
- Pistils browning gradually (some browning is desirable; rapid all-at-once browning suggests stress or pollination)
- **Lower leaf yellowing beginning — this is NORMAL senescence**
- Trichomes accumulating; clear → cloudy transition starting
- Plant heavier overall — branches may need staking

**Internal biology:**
- **Senescence beginning** — plant pulls remaining N from older leaves to support bud development; "fade" is hormonal redistribution, not deficiency
- Cannabinoid biosynthesis at peak rate
- Terpene production high
- Trichome head maturation

**Environmental targets:**
- **RH: 40–50% (aggressive drop)** — Botrytis prevention engages
- Temp: 68–75°F day, 62–68°F night (cooler nights amplify color)
- VPD: 1.2–1.5 kPa
- PPFD: 800–1000 µmol·m⁻²·s⁻¹ (some operations reduce slightly to preserve trichome integrity)
- **Direct fan-on-buds** (Tier 1 — Punja Botrytis prevention research; reduces bud-zone RH and total mold counts)

**Feed targets:**
- **Tapering EC** — reduce 0.2–0.4 EC weekly
- N reduction first; balance K and P
- Some growers begin water-only at week 7
- Cross-ref `06-nutrients-synthetic.md` flush debate (Rx Green Tech findings)

**Training allowed:**
- NONE — selective fan removal only if blocking light or showing disease
- Dying lower leaves can be removed for airflow
- Stake heavy branches with bamboo + soft ties

**Common issues:**
- **Botrytis bud rot** (the #1 late-flower disaster — cross-ref `09-disease.md`)
- Foxtailing in late flower (heat stress + genetics)
- Calyx swelling without trichome density (lower-canopy PPFD insufficient)
- Hermaphrodite "nanners" (banana-shaped male flowers in dense buds — late stress response)
- Lower-leaf yellowing **misdiagnosed as N deficiency** — this is the #1 misdiagnosis at this stage

## RIPENING (Week 8+) — Per-Stage Deep Profile

**Visible signs:**
- Most pistils brown and curled (>70%)
- Trichome color shifting: clear → cloudy → amber
- Leaves heavy-faded (yellow, purple, pink, autumn colors)
- No further bud swelling (vertical/horizontal growth stopped)
- Plant smell intensifying (terpene production peak/transitioning to volatilization)

**Internal biology:**
- Peak cannabinoid content
- THCA → CBN degradation begins (visible as amber trichomes)
- Terpene volatilization accelerates at higher temps
- Plant approaching senescence-completion
- Programmed cell death in fan leaves

**Environmental targets:**
- **RH: 40–45%** (continue Botrytis prevention)
- Temp: 65–72°F day, **60–68°F night** (cool nights amplify color expression — anthocyanin production)
- Some growers drop temp 5°F to "shock" final color
- PPFD: maintain or reduce slightly
- **Far-red EOD application** valuable here (see Far-Red EOD section)

**Feed targets:**
- Plain water OR very light feed
- Some growers do plain water last 1–2 weeks (the "flush" — see `06-nutrients-synthetic.md` for the debate)
- pH per medium

**Training allowed:**
- NONE
- Hand-removal of dying / heavily shading leaves only

**The trichome inspection cycle** (the harvest decision):
- Daily check from week 8
- Multiple bud locations (top, middle, lower)
- Calyx trichomes, not sugar leaf
- Track ratio cloudy : amber over days
- Harvest when ratio matches target effect profile
- **Hand off to `13-harvest.md`** for harvest cut, dry, cure decisions

## AUTOFLOWER FLOWERING (Different Model)

Autoflowers carry *Cannabis ruderalis* day-neutral genetics. Flowering is age-triggered, not photoperiod-triggered. The whole flowering biology is compressed and runs concurrent with continued vegetative growth.

**Lifecycle compressed:**
- Day 0–21: seedling / early veg
- Day 21–35: pre-flower onset (concurrent with continued veg growth)
- Day 35–70: full flower / bulking
- Day 70–110: ripening + harvest

**Total cycle:** 70–110 days seed-to-harvest depending on cultivar.

**Key differences from photoperiod:**
- **No flip needed** — run 18/6 (or 20/4) entire lifecycle
- **No flowering signal** — auto plants pre-flower based on age regardless of photoperiod
- Less dramatic stretch (1.5–2× typical)
- Veg growth continues into flowering — plants don't "stop" growing leaves
- Final size smaller than photoperiod equivalents
- Less training opportunity (cross-ref `10-training.md` autoflower section)

**Light schedule:**
- 18/6 standard (matches outdoor summer signal autos evolved with)
- 20/4 used by some growers — slight DLI increase
- 24/0 some growers — diminishing returns + electricity cost
- **Don't switch to 12/12** — autos don't need it; some research suggests 12/12 reduces yield in autos

**Feed / environment:**
- Generally lighter EC than photoperiod (smaller plants, lower demand)
- Same RH/temp progression but compressed timeline
- Stage transitions happen faster — environmental adjustments need to be more frequent

(Cross-ref `02-environment.md` for full physiology context.)

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Stretch (week 1–2) | 50–60% | 55–65% | 75–78°F | 65–72°F | 1.0–1.3 | Light raise weekly |

## FAR-RED EOD INTEGRATION

(Tier 1 — Bugbee Utah State CPL; Fluence Bioengineering research.)

**Far-red light (700–760 nm)** has specific applications in flowering cannabis:

| Application | Protocol | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Canopy closure (early veg) | Background far-red during light cycle | Faster canopy closure |
| **End-of-day (EOD) treatment** | **2 hours far-red after 10 hours main light** | **+10–70% cannabinoid yield reported (cultivar-dependent)** |
| Last 4 weeks pre-harvest | Increase far-red percentage in main spectrum | Boost flower density and trichome production |
| Stretch-control (mid-flower) | Minimize far-red — too much causes excess elongation | Prevents floppy stretchy plants |

**Specific Tier 1 finding (PMC research):** Northern Lights cultivar showed +70% cannabinoid yield with 2 hours far-red after 10 hours main light vs 12L control.

**Practical implementation:**
- Most modern LED fixtures include far-red diodes or accept far-red supplemental bars
- Schedule via timer: 10 hours main spectrum + 2 hours far-red only + 12 hours dark
- Or: 12 hours main + 30 min far-red at lights-off as a "sunset" cue
- Cross-ref `03-lighting.md` for spectrum selection and fixture recommendations

**Caveat:** too much far-red at the wrong time = excessive stem elongation (the "shade avoidance response"). Use targeted, not constant.

## Common Misdiagnosis Traps

| Looks like | Actually | How to tell |
|---|---|---|
| **N deficiency** in week 7+ | **Normal senescence (N redistribution)** | Lower-leaf yellowing only; upper canopy healthy = senescence; uniform yellowing reaching upper canopy = real def. **#1 misdiagnosis at this stage.** |
| Foxtailing | Heat stress OR genetic | Genetic foxtail = consistent across plant + matches strain reputation; stress foxtail = top canopy near light + reversed in cooler conditions |
| Hermaphroditism | Light leak OR genetic predisposition OR heat stress | Check dark cycle integrity first; some genetics hermie under any stress |
| Pistils browning early | Heat stress OR pollination OR genuine ripening | Heat = top buds first; pollination = seeds visible; ripening = even progression with trichome maturation |
| Stretched plant beyond expectation | Sativa genetics underestimated | Check breeder description; pure sativa stretch can be 4× |
| "Buds aren't fattening" | Insufficient PPFD OR low EC OR strain genetics | PPFD <600 µmol = under-lit; EC <1.8 mid-flower = under-fed; otherwise check strain |
| Trichomes still all clear at week 8 | Cultivar with longer flower cycle | Some sativas need 12–14 weeks; check breeder timeline |
| Light bleach top buds | LED too close OR too high PPFD | Raise light immediately; bleached calyxes won't recover |
| Brown spots on calyxes | Botrytis OR thrips damage OR nute burn | Botrytis = fuzzy mycelium when broken open; thrips = silver streak + frass; nute burn = tip-only |
| Single calyx browning early | Pin rot (genetic) OR early Botrytis | Pin rot = isolated, doesn't spread; Botrytis = spreads rapidly |
| Reduced bud density at lower nodes | Insufficient PPFD penetration | Lollipop next time; defoliation; raise lower buds via training |

## Verification Questions Before Major Flowering Decisions

1. Stage and week-in-stage?
2. Photoperiod confirmed (12/12 or alternate, no light leak)?
3. Strain expected stretch multiplier and ceiling clearance?
4. Pre-flip plant height vs current height (validate stretch math)?
5. Environmental setpoints actual vs target (RH especially)?
6. Feed schedule current and matching stage?
7. Training cuts in last 2 weeks (recovery status)?
8. Trichome status if past week 6 (don't guess; check)?
9. Pest/disease pressure (Botrytis especially in late flower)?
10. Harvest timing planned (back-calculate dry/cure schedule)?

If 4+ unknown → don't make major decisions yet. Get the data.
