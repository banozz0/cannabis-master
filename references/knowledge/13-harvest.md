# 13 — Harvest

Harvest is the hinge between flowering biology and post-harvest chemistry. The decisions made in the 48 hours before and during the cut lock in flaws that drying and curing cannot fix — over-mature trichomes degrade to CBN, under-mature flower has weak potency, mold-infected buds spread through dry rooms. Cross-reference with `12-flowering.md` (this file's harvest decision is the output of the ripening section's trichome inspection), `14-drying.md` (the drying environment must be ready BEFORE the cut, not after), `15-curing.md` (cure plan affects whether to wet or dry trim), `16-trimming.md` (trim technique deep dive), `09-disease.md` (Botrytis at harvest = isolation protocol mandatory; mold testing implications for commercial), `02-environment.md` (drying environment specifications), `references/workflows/harvest-readiness.md` (the workflow that uses this file's decision data), `references/state/grow-log.md` (yield tracking entries).

## How to Use This File

1. **Confirm harvest readiness** via the multi-factor decision (trichomes + pistils + calyx + strain + environment + logistics) — don't harvest on a single signal.
2. **Set the drying environment 24–48 hours pre-cut** — RH 55–62%, temp 60–68°F, dim/dark, gentle airflow. Verify with hygrometer before cut day.
3. **Choose wet trim or dry trim path** before the cut — they require different equipment and labor.
4. **Execute cut on schedule** — early morning or late evening preferred (cooler temps preserve terpenes).
5. **Post-cut handle within 2 hours** — get plants from cut to drying environment fast; don't stack wet plants; don't leave in sun.
6. **Yield-track at every stage** — wet untrimmed → wet trimmed → dry untrimmed → dry trimmed → final cured for grow-log.
7. **Hand off to `14-drying.md`** for the dry phase.

## Critical Concept — Harvest Window Decision Math

Harvest readiness is multi-factor. Single-factor decisions (pistil-only, week-count-only, trichome-only) consistently produce suboptimal harvests.

| Factor | Read | Weight in decision |
|---|---|---|
| **Trichome ratio** (cross-ref `12-flowering.md`) | 80–90% cloudy + 10–20% amber for balanced; sativa-leaning earlier; indica-heavy later | **Primary — driver of harvest timing** |
| **Pistil browning** | 70–90% browned/curled at typical harvest; pistils brown earlier than trichomes mature, so pistil-only = early harvest | Secondary signal, not primary |
| **Calyx swelling** | Mature calyxes are plump, fully resin-coated, almost "shiny" | Confirms general readiness; not timing |
| **Strain expected timing** | Breeder description (sativas 12–14+ weeks, indicas 7–9 weeks); your grow journal across cycles | Sanity-check; if trichomes say week 14 and breeder says week 8, recheck the inspection |
| **Environmental window** | Outdoor weather, indoor schedule, mold pressure | Logistics override; harvest early to avoid crop loss |
| **Effect profile target** | Sativa-leaning earlier (50–70% cloudy, no amber); balanced (80% cloudy / 20% amber); indica-heavy (30%+ amber) | User preference within trichome range |
| **Logistics** | Drying space ready, trim help available, time committed | Confirm readiness; don't cut without infrastructure |

(Tier 3 — Ed Rosenthal *Judging Cannabis Ripeness by Trichomes* primary source for the trichome-driven framework; Tier 2 — Cannabis Business Times harvest articles.)

**The sativa/indica harvest fork:**
- Sativa effects depend on intact THC (less degradation to CBN); harvest at peak cloudy with minimal amber
- Indica effects benefit from CBN content (more sedating); harvest with 20–30%+ amber
- Hybrid: 80/20 cloudy/amber is the standard "balanced" window

## Critical Concept — Wet Trim vs Dry Trim Fork

This decision cascades through the entire post-harvest pipeline. Pick before the cut.

| Aspect | Wet Trim | Dry Trim |
|---|---|---|
| When trimmed | Immediately after cut, before drying | After 7–14 day dry, before cure |
| Difficulty | Easier — leaves stiff and snip cleanly | Harder — leaves brittle, crumble; slower |
| Drying speed | Faster (less plant material to dry) | Slower (full plant material retains moisture) |
| **Terpene retention** | **Lower** — leaf cuts release volatiles; trichomes more exposed | **Higher** — fan/sugar leaves act as protective barrier slowing terpene evaporation |
| Cannabinoid retention | Slightly lower (more trichome handling) | Slightly higher (less mechanical handling pre-dry) |
| Bag appeal | Crisper, cleaner final look | Slightly more "natural" look; some sugar-leaf shadows on calyxes |
| Space required | Less drying space (just buds + small stems) | More drying space (whole plant or branches) |
| Trim time per pound | ~30–60 min per lb wet | ~60–90 min per lb dry |
| Best for | Commercial high-volume; controlled environments | Craft, terp-focused, hash/extract destination |

(Tier 2/3 — multiple commercial trim service comparisons; Tier 3 — community consensus across grower forums and trim-industry sources.)

**Decision rules:**
- **High-throughput commercial** → wet trim (efficiency wins; small terpene loss acceptable for scale)
- **Smoke quality / terpene-driven craft** → dry trim
- **Hash / extract destination** → dry trim almost always (terpenes critical for product value; trichomes more delicate when wet)
- **Small home grow** → either works; many growers do "rough wet trim" (fan leaves only, immediately) + "final dry trim" (sugar leaves after dry) hybrid for best of both
- **Botrytis-affected plants** → wet trim mandatory (need to cut around damage immediately; can't wait for dry)

## Critical Concept — The Harvest Day Timeline

Harvest is labor-intensive. Underestimating time → rushed work → quality loss.

| Plant count | Wet trim path | Dry trim path |
|---|---|---|
| 1 plant (5-gal mature) | 2–4 hours | 1–2 hours cut + hang |
| 4 plants (4×4 tent) | 8–16 hours | 4–8 hours cut + hang |
| 10 plants | Full day + helpers | Half day + helpers |
| Commercial run | Multi-day staged operation | Multi-day staged operation |

**Schedule reality:**
- Environmental prep happens 24–48h before, not day-of
- All tools, bins, drying space, scale ready before first cut
- Cool morning preferred (terpene preservation; less stress on trimmer)
- Don't start at 2 PM on a hot day expecting to finish by dinner

## PRE-HARVEST PREPARATION (1–2 Weeks Before Cut)

### Final Environmental Shift (Last Week of Flower)

Cross-ref `12-flowering.md` Ripening section for full progression.
- RH: 40–45% (Botrytis prevention engaged aggressively)
- Temp day: 65–72°F
- Temp night: 60–68°F (cool nights amplify color expression — anthocyanins)
- Some commercial drops temp 5°F in last 7 days to "shock" final color
- Direct fan-on-buds (Tier 1 — Punja research; reduces bud-zone RH and total mold)

### Drying Space Preparation (24–48 Hours Pre-Cut)

(Cross-ref `14-drying.md` for full drying protocol.)
- RH: 55–62% target (Boveda 62% is the standard)
- Temp: 60–68°F
- Light: dim or dark (UV degrades cannabinoids)
- Airflow: gentle, indirect (no direct fan on plants)
- Test hygrometer reading **before cut day** — adjust dehumidifier/humidifier as needed
- Capacity: hanging space + 4–6 inches between plants; trim line + bin space if wet trim path

### Equipment Checklist

**Cutting:**
- Sharp pruners (large cuts, main stem)
- Multiple scissors / micro-tip trimmers (clog faster than expected — have 4–6 ready to swap)
- Isopropyl alcohol (70%+) for tool cleaning between batches and HLVd-conscious work
- Sterile if commercial (10% bleach soak between plants for HLVd cross-prevention — see `09-disease.md`)

**Handling:**
- Nitrile gloves — multiple boxes (get sticky fast)
- Glove changes between plants if HLVd-conscious
- Large bins for wet plant material
- Trim trays (with screen for kief collection)
- Drying rack OR hanger lines + clothespins / clamps
- Strong twine for hanging branches

**Tracking:**
- Scale (1g resolution minimum; 0.1g better)
- Notebook or grow-log entries
- Camera for plant documentation pre-cut

### Schedule Prep

- Trim help arranged if needed (notify 1 week in advance)
- Trim space cleared of other tasks
- No other major commitments day-of
- Music / podcast / movie queued (trimming is meditative for some, mind-numbing for others)

## THE HARVEST CUT — Multiple Approaches

### Single-Cut (Whole Plant Down at Once)

**When to use:**
- Home grower with manageable plant count
- All buds at similar maturity (good canopy training pays off here)
- Logistics demand it (one-day window available)
- Outdoor with weather pressure incoming

**Execute:**
1. Cut at base of main stem with pruners
2. Hang whole plant from drying line by stem (if dry-trim path) OR cut into large branches (if wet-trim or smaller drying space)
3. Branches by branch into wet trim line if wet-trim path
4. Continue with all plants in same approach

**Pros:** simple scheduling; clear "harvest day"; one decision point.

**Cons:** forces commitment to all-cut readiness; lower-canopy buds may be less mature than tops; high labor concentration.

### Staged / Progressive Cut (Top Buds First, Lowers Later)

**When to use:**
- Lower-canopy buds noticeably less mature than top
- Want to spread single plant's harvest across 5–14 days
- Indoor grower who can keep plants flowering after partial cut
- Maximizing per-plant yield (Tier 2/3 consensus: up to 30% more total bud weight via staging)

**Execute:**
1. Day 0: cut top 1/3 of plant (hands or pruners; cut individual top branches)
2. Leave rest of plant in flowering room with same light/environment
3. Day 5–7: cut middle 1/3 (now better-lit and continued maturing)
4. Day 10–14: cut bottom 1/3
5. Each cut goes to drying immediately

**Pros:** optimizes maturity at each canopy level; lower buds get full light after tops removed and continue developing; reported 1.6× yield increase at higher PPFD with staged harvest in some studies.

**Cons:** more handling events; longer flowering room occupation; **mold risk if RH high** (cuts on remaining plant are entry points).

**Caveat:** if humidity problem exists, single-cut is safer — staged cuts on remaining plants invite Botrytis at the cut sites.

### Branch-by-Branch (Commercial)

**When to use:**
- Large operations (50+ plants per harvest event)
- Staff trim line workflow
- Conveyor / processing line integration

**Execute:**
1. Cut individual branches from plants
2. Load onto carts or conveyors
3. Move to bucking station (machine removes branches from buds)
4. Trim line (manual or automated trimmer at 20–40 lbs/hour throughput)
5. Direct to drying or wet-trim packaging

**Pros:** scales to commercial volume; trim line efficiency; integrated equipment workflows.

**Cons:** requires capital equipment; staged drying space; standard operating procedures and trained staff.

(Tier 2 — Mobius, Triminator, Twister commercial trim equipment specifications; Cannabis Business Times commercial harvest workflow articles.)

### Outdoor Weather-Window Harvest

**When to use:**
- Outdoor / greenhouse cultivation
- Frost forecast / heavy rain incoming
- Mold pressure rising rapidly (Botrytis blooms in 24–48h wet conditions)

**Execute:**
1. Emergency whole-plant cut even if not at peak ripeness
2. Better early than mold-loss
3. Bring plants under cover immediately
4. Drying environment may be makeshift but must be set up

**Pros:** salvages crop from weather damage.

**Cons:** suboptimal trichome ratio; rushed processing.

**Decision:** if rain forecast >0.5 inch and humidity >70% sustained → cut immediately, don't wait.

## WET TRIM EXECUTION

1. Cut plant at base; bring to trim station
2. Remove fan leaves first (large, easy to grab; don't waste scissor edge on these)
3. Remove sugar leaves around calyxes — close trim for crisp look, "loose" trim leaves more leaf for terpene preservation
4. Branch-by-branch: trim, set on drying rack
5. Save trim for hash / edibles / bubble bag (bag separately from buds)
6. Stem material to compost or trash
7. Move trimmed buds to drying environment within 2 hours

**Tip:** swap scissors every 30–45 minutes — resin clogs the joints and slows you down. Soak used scissors in isopropyl while using fresh pair.

## DRY TRIM EXECUTION

1. Cut plant or branches; hang in drying environment
2. Wait 7–14 days for proper dry (cross-ref `14-drying.md` for completion criteria)
3. Test "snap" — small stems should snap, not bend
4. Begin trim: branch-by-branch
5. Fan leaves often pre-removed (they crumble when fully dry; pre-remove at hang time)
6. Sugar leaves trim with sharper scissors (more brittle; cleaner cut)
7. Move trimmed buds to cure jars immediately

**Tip:** dry trim is harder physically — sit, take breaks, manage hand fatigue. A long trim session in dry can cause repetitive strain.

## POST-CUT HANDLING (Time-Sensitive)

Once cut, plants are vulnerable. Get them to a controlled drying environment fast.

| Threat | Window | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Mold growth | Hours in warm/wet conditions | Drying environment ready pre-cut; don't stack wet plants |
| Terpene loss | Continuous from cut moment | Cool ambient; dim/dark; minimize handling time |
| Cannabinoid degradation | UV-driven | No direct sun; dim transport |
| Trichome shatter | Mechanical | Gentle handling; don't shake or drop branches |

**Hard rules:**
- Get from cut → drying environment within 2 hours
- Don't stack wet plants (anaerobic pockets = mold)
- Don't leave in direct sun (UV degrades cannabinoids; heat volatilizes terpenes)
- Don't leave in 80°F+ ambient (mold acceleration; terpene loss)
- Hang plants spaced 4–6 inches apart for airflow
- Dim or no light during drying (UV degrades cannabinoids over time)
- Standard target: dark + ventilated + 60°F + 60% RH

## YIELD ESTIMATION — Wet:Dry Ratios

Tracking weight at each stage is essential for grow-log and forecasting future grows.

| Stage | Typical reading | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Wet plant (full, including stems) | Set baseline | Heavy; mostly water |
| Wet trim (just buds + small stems) | 30–50% of wet plant | After fan leaves removed |
| **Dry untrimmed (after 7–14 day dry)** | **~22–25% of wet plant weight** | Rule of thumb: 4:1 to 5:1 wet:dry |
| Dry trimmed (final flower) | 70–80% of dry untrimmed | After sugar-leaf trim |
| Cured (after 2–8 weeks cure) | Slight loss continues; 95%+ of dry trimmed | Final tracking weight |

**Standard rule of thumb (Tier 2/3 — multiple sources):** **wet plant weight × 0.20–0.25 = approximate dry trimmed weight.** A 4-lb wet plant typically yields ~1 lb of dry trimmed flower.

**Variability:** strain genetics, drying conditions, trimming aggressiveness, ambient humidity at harvest all affect ratio. Track over multiple grows to dial in your facility's typical ratio.

**For grow-log:**
```
- Wet plant weight (full): X g
- Wet plant weight (post fan-leaf removal): X g
- Dry untrimmed: X g
- Dry trimmed (final): X g
- Cured: X g (after Y days cure)
- Wet:dry ratio: X:1
- Trim/total ratio: X%
```

## STAGED HARVEST PROTOCOL — Deeper Dive

For growers committed to maximizing per-plant yield and quality:

**Pre-conditions:**
- Plants with significant canopy depth gradient (top vs bottom maturity differs)
- Indoor or greenhouse with continued lighting available
- Low humidity environment (RH <55%) to minimize mold risk on cut plants
- Time and labor available across 2–3 weeks

**3-stage standard:**

| Stage | Day | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Cut top 1/3 of plant when top buds at target trichome ratio |
| 2 | Day 5–7 | Cut middle 1/3 (now receiving full light, accelerated maturity) |
| 3 | Day 10–14 | Cut bottom 1/3 (final maturity catch-up) |

**Why it works:**
- Top buds removed → light penetrates deeper to lower canopy
- Lower buds receive PPFD they couldn't access before
- Cannabinoid biosynthesis genes still upregulated → catch-up bulking + trichome production
- Some research reports 1.6× yield increase under higher PPFD with staged harvest

**Risk management:**
- Cut surfaces are mold entry points — RH discipline mandatory (<55%)
- Sterile pruners (HLVd + general sanitation)
- Cover or seal cuts if humidity rises (rare; usually unnecessary indoor)

## DEBATED PRE-HARVEST PRACTICES (Honest Evidence)

These are widely-practiced techniques with mixed evidence. Honest framing:

### 24–48 Hour Dark Period Pre-Harvest

**Claim:** trichome production boost; terpene preservation; THC increase.

**Evidence:**
- One often-cited study (SIMM / TNO Laboratories / University of Leiden) reportedly found controlled dark stress 48–72h could increase THC up to 30% — primary source not widely available; specifics contested
- Counter-evidence: cited testing on 30 strains found only 4 showed higher trichome concentration; 26 showed hermaphroditism or malformed buds (Tier 3 — *Cultivating Cannabis* book reference)
- Other research shows minimal effect

**Honest framing:** limited peer-reviewed support; widely practiced; mixed results across genetics; low downside if dark cycle done correctly. **Treat as optional, not protocol.**

### Ice Water Root Flush 24h Pre-Harvest

**Claim:** trichomes "crystallize" or increase from cold shock.

**Evidence:** **No peer-reviewed support.** Folk technique; subjective grower reports.

**Risk:** cold shock can stress plants; root zone temps too low cause uptake issues; not validated.

**Honest framing:** community-consensus technique without scientific support. Skip unless you have personal A/B data.

### Lights-Off / Morning Harvest

**Claim:** terpenes most volatile in light/heat; harvesting at lights-off or cool morning preserves them.

**Evidence:** terpene volatility well-documented in plant science generally; harvest-time effect not isolated in cannabis-specific research, but the general principle (cooler = less volatilization) is sound.

**Honest framing:** reasonable practice with low downside. Cool morning harvest is plausibly beneficial.

### Pre-Harvest Flush (1–2 Weeks Plain Water)

(Cross-ref `06-nutrients-synthetic.md` flush debate.)

**Claim:** smoother smoke; cleaner ash; better taste.

**Evidence:** Rx Green Technologies study (Tier 1/2): no yield, potency, terpene, or taste benefit from flushing; zero-day flush slightly preferred in taste panel.

**Honest framing:** the Rx Green data suggests pre-harvest flush is unnecessary. "Tapering" (gradual EC reduction) makes sense; full plain-water flush doesn't have evidence.

### "Bend the Stem" Pre-Cut Stress

**Claim:** partially break main stem 24–48h pre-cut → stress response → more trichomes.

**Evidence:** anecdotal; no controlled studies.

**Risk:** if too aggressive, plant tips over; reduces airflow; mold risk.

**Honest framing:** unsupported folk technique; risk outweighs unproven benefit.

## Botrytis at Harvest — Isolation Protocol

(Cross-ref `09-disease.md` for full Botrytis biology.)

If Botrytis (gray mold / bud rot) found during harvest:

1. **Stop and isolate.** Do not continue cutting healthy plants until infected material handled.
2. **Cut affected sections** with sterile pruners — cut at least 3 cm BELOW visible infection (Tier 2 — multiple disease management sources)
3. **Sterilize tools between cuts** with 70%+ isopropyl OR 10% bleach
4. **Dispose in sealed bag** away from healthy material; off-premises if possible
5. **Drop drying-room RH below 40%** to inhibit Botrytis spread on remaining buds
6. **Inspect every dried bud** during dry phase — Botrytis can appear post-cut
7. **Don't compost** infected material on premises (spores persist)
8. **Test if commercial** — qPCR can detect Botrytis at sub-symptomatic levels

**Hard rule:** Botrytis-affected wet plants cannot be dry-trimmed safely. Wet-trim removes affected tissue immediately; dry-trim risks spread during drying.

## Common Mistakes & Misdiagnosis Traps

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Harvest on pistil-only signal | Premature harvest, weak potency | Always confirm with trichome inspection |
| Harvest by week count alone | Genetic variation missed | Inspect; some strains 12+ weeks |
| Drying environment not ready at cut time | Mold or rapid over-dry | Set up 24–48h pre-cut; verify with hygrometer |
| Wet-trim into bins with no airflow | Mold within hours | Rack or hang immediately; max 2 hours in bins |
| Direct sun on cut plants | Terpene + cannabinoid loss | Cover, transport in shade |
| Dirty tools | Resin buildup, cross-contamination, HLVd transmission | Clean per HLVd protocol |
| Trim leaves mixed with bud | Final product quality drops | Separate streams: bud, sugar trim, fan trim, stem |
| Yield optimism | Disappointment from 4:1 ratio surprise | Track by stage; learn your facility's ratio |
| Outdoor harvest delay through rain | Botrytis blooms 24–48h | Cut pre-rain even if early |
| Botrytis-affected plants dry-trimmed | Mold spreads through drying room | Wet-trim mandatory for affected material |
| Cutting in 2 PM heat | Terpene loss, stressed trimmer | Schedule cool morning |
| Underestimating trim time | Quality drops at end of fatigue | Plan realistic hours; recruit help |

## Verification Questions Before Cutting

1. Trichome ratio confirmed via microscope/loupe (multiple bud sites)?
2. Pistil browning percentage assessed?
3. Drying space ready (RH/temp/airflow tested with hygrometer)?
4. Wet trim vs dry trim path decided?
5. Equipment ready (scissors x 4–6, gloves, bins, scale, drying rack)?
6. Time blocked (4–8+ hours per 4 plants)?
7. Help arranged if needed?
8. Yield baseline tracking plan ready (weights at each stage)?
9. Disease pressure assessed (Botrytis isolation protocol prepped)?
10. Strain notes ready to capture for next-grow planning (final phenotype observations)?
11. Trim destination planned (hash, edibles, waste streams separated)?
12. Sterile tool protocol (HLVd-conscious between plants)?

If 4+ unknown → don't cut yet. Get the data and the plan in place.
