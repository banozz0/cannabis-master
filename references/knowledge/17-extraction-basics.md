# 17 — Extraction Basics

Educational overview of cannabis concentrate production. Covers solventless methods (dry sift, ice water hash, rosin) at operational depth, solvent-based methods (BHO/PHO, ethanol, CO2, distillation) at principle and safety depth only, plus a decarboxylation primer.

This file is a how-to for solventless work and a how-it-works for solvent work. Hydrocarbon extraction is industrial chemistry — this file does not provide blasting, purge, or recovery recipes.

## How to Use This File

1. Identify your goal (preserve full spectrum, isolate cannabinoids, vape, edibles, dabs)
2. Match goal to method via decision tree at the bottom
3. Read principle of chosen method to understand what you're separating and why
4. Review safety section — non-negotiable for any solvent work
5. Equipment specs only matter once method is decided — don't buy hardware first

## Core Concept — The Extraction Spectrum

All extraction is the same problem: separate trichome heads (where cannabinoids and terpenes live) from plant material, then optionally isolate molecules. Methods differ in HOW.

| Axis | Spectrum |
|---|---|
| Mechanism | Mechanical (sift, water, pressure) → Chemical (hydrocarbon, ethanol, CO2) |
| Selectivity | Full-spectrum (kief, hash, rosin) → Refined (distillate, isolate) |
| Solvent residual | Zero (solventless) → Trace (purged BHO) → ppm regulated |
| Skill barrier | Low (dry sift) → Extreme (closed-loop, distillation) |
| Capital cost | $50 (bubble bags) → $500k+ (commercial closed-loop + lab) |
| Legal/safety | Generally fine at home → Felony explosion risk (open blasting hydrocarbons) |

**Decision principle:** start solventless. Top-tier solventless concentrates (live rosin, full-melt hash) match or exceed solvent extracts in flavor and potency, and don't require a licensed facility.

## Core Concept — GIGO (Garbage In, Garbage Out)

Extraction concentrates everything — cannabinoids, terpenes, AND contaminants. Bad starting material makes a bad extract regardless of method.

What carries through extraction:
- **Pesticide / fungicide residues** — concentrated proportionally
- **Mold spores / mycotoxins** — survive most non-distillation methods
- **Plant lipids and chlorophyll** — appear as green/dark color, harsh smoke
- **Heavy metals** — concentrate into oil
- **Microbial contamination** — survives solventless cleanly

Quality starting material requirements:
- Frozen-fresh (for live extracts) OR properly dried/cured (for traditional)
- Pesticide-free (test if commercial)
- No PM, botrytis, or aspergillus
- Trichome maturity matched to goal (cloudy peak for THC; amber for sedating; capitates for live extracts)
- Trim/popcorn fine for hash; nug or popcorn for rosin

For commercial: COA on input material before extraction is mandatory.

## DECARBOXYLATION (Decarb) Primer

Decarboxylation = removing a CO2 group from acidic cannabinoid molecules to convert them to neutral, orally-active forms. Required for edibles; partial or skipped for inhalation.

### Why it matters

- THCa is **not** psychoactive; THC is. Smoking decarbs in real-time at combustion.
- Edibles need decarbed material — raw flower in butter ≠ active edible.
- Vape concentrates partially decarb during vaping; full pre-decarb flattens flavor.
- Some products WANT THCa intact (THCa diamonds for dabbing — heat decarbs at consumption).

### Decarb time/temp curve (cannabis flower)

Approximate (Wang et al., Citti et al., Tier 1):

| Temperature | Time | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 105°C / 220°F | 90 min | Slow, preserves more terpenes |
| 115°C / 240°F | 45 min | Balanced — common edible default |
| 125°C / 257°F | 25 min | Faster, terp loss accelerates |
| 145°C / 293°F | 7 min | Fast, heavy terp + cannabinoid loss |

Higher temps = more terpene loss + risk of converting THC → CBN (sedating, less psychoactive). For terp preservation, lower-and-longer always wins.

### Decarb in concentrates vs flower

- **Hash, rosin, kief** — partial decarb during extraction is normal; full decarb requires post-processing bake
- **Distillate** — already decarbed during distillation (high heat)
- **THCa diamonds** — un-decarbed by design
- **RSO (Rick Simpson Oil)** — fully decarbed for oral medical use

### Practical guidance

- Edibles from flower: 240°F for 40 min, then infuse
- Edibles from rosin/hash: 240°F for 25-30 min in a sealed jar (preserves more terps)
- Vape carts: distillate is auto-decarbed; rosin needs no further decarb (vape coil handles it)

## SOLVENTLESS METHODS

No chemical solvents introduced. Mechanical or thermal separation of trichomes from plant material.

### Method 1 — Dry Sift (Kief)

**Principle:** Vibrate or rub frozen, dry flower over fine mesh; brittle trichome heads break off and fall through; plant matter stays above.

**Equipment:**
- Three-chamber pollen box ($30-100) — beginner
- Tumbler (Sasquatch, Pollinator Twister) — intermediate, $400-1500
- Static-tech sieve (DryWasher style) — advanced, larger volumes
- Mesh micron sizes: typical stack is 70µ / 90µ / 120µ / 150µ

**Process overview:**
1. Freeze flower 24-48h at -18°C minimum (-20°C+ ideal)
2. Cold work surface (chilled marble, frozen tray)
3. Sieve through descending mesh sizes
4. Collect by mesh layer — finer mesh = purer kief

**Quality grades (informal star scale):**
- 1-3 star = green-tinted, won't melt cleanly, smoke/edibles only
- 4-5 star = blonde, partially melts when heated
- 6 star = full-melt, bubbles cleanly to clear oil on parchment, dabbable

**Yield:** 5-15% of dry flower weight depending on quality and freshness

**Common errors:**
- Working at room temp — trichomes smear instead of breaking off
- Over-sieving — drives plant matter through, lowers purity
- Using sticky mid-cure flower — won't release trichomes; needs to be drier

### Method 2 — Ice Water Hash (Bubble Hash)

**Principle:** Cold water makes trichome heads brittle; agitation breaks them off the plant; nested mesh bags filter heads from contaminants and plant matter by size.

**Equipment:**
- Bubble bag set (typically 8 bags, 25-220µ) — $80-200
- Wash machine (Bubblebag Dude, M1, Osprey) — $300-2000 for serious work
- Freeze dryer (Harvest Right standard) — $2500-5000, **strongly recommended for premium grade** — freeze press technique (parchment + freezer) works for personal-scale but loses melt and risks microbial growth
- Quality water (RO or filtered, no chlorine)
- Ice — 2-3x the weight of starting material

**Process overview (high level):**
1. Freeze fresh flower (live wash) or use cured trim/popcorn
2. Combine with ice and cold water in wash vessel
3. Agitate 8-25 min depending on material and goal
4. Drain through stacked bags from largest micron (top) to smallest
5. Collect by bag — 73-120µ is the prized fraction
6. Freeze-dry collected hash 24-48h at -40°C plate temp

**Bag micron logic:**
- 220µ — coarse contaminants and plant matter — discard
- 160µ — large stalked trichomes, plant fines — second tier
- **120µ, 90µ, 73µ** — premium head-only fractions
- 45µ, 25µ — small heads, broken material — work-down for rosin

**Quality grades (star scale, melt-based):**
- 6-star bubble = full-melt, "ice cream" texture, dabbable solo
- 5-star = press to rosin or smoke
- Lower stars = press to rosin (work-down) or cook into edibles

**Live vs cured starting material:**
- Live = fresh-frozen at harvest, terps preserved → "live hash"
- Cured = dried/cured flower → less terps but often higher pure yield

**Yield:** 2-8% by dry weight (1-3% if calculating from fresh-frozen wet weight)

**Common errors:**
- Air-drying instead of freeze-drying (ruins terps, microbial risk, melts onto parchment)
- Chlorinated water (kills terpenes)
- Over-agitation (drives plant matter through, lowers purity)
- Insufficient pre-freeze (-18°C+ for 48h before)
- Working with insufficient ice — temp rises above ~4°C and trichomes get sticky

### Method 3 — Rosin

**Principle:** Heat + mechanical pressure squeezes terpene/cannabinoid resin out of starting material through a micron filter bag. No solvents.

**Three classes by input:**
- **Flower rosin** — pressed from cured flower directly. Lower yield, entry point.
- **Hash rosin** — pressed from bubble hash. Higher yield, cleaner, premium.
- **Live rosin** — pressed from fresh-frozen-then-washed bubble hash. Top-tier, full live terp preservation.

**Equipment:**
- Rosin press: pneumatic ($600-3000), hydraulic ($1500-6000), commercial ($10k+)
- Heated plates with separate top/bottom temp control
- Pressure: 200-1500 psi at the bag (NOT machine PSI — machine PSI is hydraulic line pressure, not what the bag sees)
- Micron bags (rosin filter bags): 25µ, 37µ, 72µ, 90µ, 120µ
- Parchment paper (food-grade, unbleached)
- Optional: cold collection plate (chilled steel for clean catch)

**Press parameters by input:**

| Input | Temp range | Bag PSI | Time | Bag micron |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Flower (high-temp) | 180-200°F | 800-1200 | 60-90s | 90-120µ |
| Flower (low-temp) | 165-180°F | 800-1200 | 2-4 min | 90-120µ |
| Bubble hash (cured) | 180-200°F | 600-900 | 45-90s | 25-37µ |
| Bubble hash (live) | 160-180°F | 400-700 | 1-3 min | 25-37µ |
| Dry sift | 170-190°F | 600-900 | 60-120s | 25-37µ |

Lower temp = better terp preservation but lower yield. Higher temp = more yield but terp degradation.

**Yield:**
- Flower → 15-25% (premium flower can hit 30%)
- Bubble hash → 60-80%
- Live hash → 60-75%
- Dry sift → 70-85%

**Post-press product textures (what you do with fresh rosin):**
- **Fresh press / sap / shatter** — straight off the press, glassy or runny depending on input
- **Badder / batter** — whipped at 70-90°F to homogenize into creamy paste
- **Cold cure** — sealed jar at 50-60°F for 1-3 weeks; texture stabilizes, terps integrate into matrix
- **Hot cure / "jam"** — sealed cure at 80-90°F; faster, glossier, more terp aroma but more degradation risk
- **Diamonds and sauce** — extreme cure where THCa crystallizes from terp-rich sauce (rare from rosin, common from sauce-style BHO)

**Common errors:**
- Pressing too hot — chlorophyll and lipids leak through, dark green color, harsh
- Wrong micron bag — too coarse leaks plant fines, too fine prevents flow / blowouts
- Overpacking bag — restricts flow, blowouts at edges
- Cold/hot curing without proper temp control — failed cure, runny mess or grainy separation

### Method 4 — Other Solventless

- **Charas / hand-rubbed hash** — traditional Indian method; live plants rubbed by hand, resin collected on palms; legal in restricted regions only
- **Roll-press kief** — kief into a pollen press for hash blocks, traditional dry hash
- **Heat press / "moonrocks"** — flower coated in oil, rolled in kief; not extraction, just product
- **Mechanical sieve like Bubbleator** — water + agitation but no nested bags; less selective

## SOLVENT-BASED METHODS (Educational Overview)

⚠️ **Solvent extraction is industrial chemistry.** Hydrocarbon and supercritical CO2 setups are NOT home projects. Open-blasting BHO causes hundreds of explosions per year in the US. This section explains principles for understanding what you're consuming or working with in a licensed facility — NOT a how-to.

### Method 1 — Hydrocarbon (BHO / PHO)

**Solvents:** n-butane, isobutane, propane, blends.

**Principle:** Liquefied hydrocarbon (boiling point -1°C butane, -42°C propane) passes through frozen biomass in a sealed pressurized vessel. Cannabinoids and terpenes dissolve into solvent. Solvent is recovered and recycled; oil is collected and purged of residual solvent under vacuum.

**Equipment (commercial-only context):**
- Closed-loop extractor — stainless steel, ASME-rated, $5k-100k+
- Solvent recovery pump and condenser system
- Vacuum oven for purging — controlled below boiling point of solvent
- Cold trap, dewaxing column for filtration
- C1D1 booth required (Class 1 Division 1 explosion-proof room) for legal commercial operation
- Residual solvent testing via gas chromatography at lab partner

**Why butane vs propane:**
- **Butane** (BP -1°C) — better at pulling waxes/lipids; produces shatter, batter, sap
- **Propane** (BP -42°C) — selective for terpenes/cannabinoids; produces sauce, diamonds, live resin
- **Blends** (e.g. 70/30 butane/propane) — most commercial live resin

**Common products:**
- **Shatter** — fully purged, glassy translucent slab
- **Wax / crumble** — agitated during purge, opaque
- **Live resin** — fresh-frozen input, high terp retention, runny/saucy
- **Diamonds and sauce** — THCa crystallizes from terp-rich sauce
- **Sugar / sauce / badder** — varying textures via purge technique

**Quality determinants:**
- Starting material freshness (fresh-frozen for live resin)
- Solvent purity (n-butane 99.5%+ vs lighter-fluid impurities)
- Residual solvent ppm (state-legal limit typically <5000ppm; quality producers <500ppm)
- Color (light = clean; dark = chlorophyll, lipids, plant matter pulled)

**Safety reality:**
- Butane vapor is heavier than air; pools in low spots
- Static electricity ignites; non-conductive plastic forbidden
- Open-blasting (passing solvent through tube into open container) is illegal nearly everywhere and lethal regularly
- Even closed-loop systems require explosion-proof rooms, fire suppression, gas detection, and operator training

### Method 2 — Ethanol

**Principle:** Cold ethanol washes biomass briefly; cannabinoids and terps dissolve; ethanol is then evaporated under vacuum and recovered for reuse.

- **Cold (-40°C+) ethanol** — selective, less chlorophyll pulled
- **Warm/room-temp ethanol** — pulls chlorophyll and waxes; requires winterization (re-dissolve in ethanol, freeze, filter waxes)

**Equipment:**
- Centrifuge extractor (Delta Separations CUP, Capna) — $50k+
- Rotary evaporator (rotovap) — $3k-15k for solvent recovery
- Dewaxing equipment (filter papers, vacuum)
- Falling-film evaporator (commercial scale)

**Common products:**
- **RSO (Rick Simpson Oil)** — full-extract oil, fully decarbed, oral/topical medical use
- **Tinctures** — diluted in carrier ethanol or food oil
- **Crude oil** → distillate substrate
- **Vape cart base** (after distillation)

**Pros/cons:**
- Pro: safer than hydrocarbons (flammable but not explosive at typical concentrations), large-scale capable, full-spectrum capable cold
- Con: pulls chlorophyll if not cold enough; ethanol expensive at scale; recovery never 100%

### Method 3 — CO2 (Supercritical / Subcritical)

**Principle:** Above 31°C and 73 bar pressure, CO2 enters the supercritical phase — behaves as both liquid and gas. Tunable selectivity by adjusting temperature and pressure to extract different fractions.

- **Subcritical CO2** (cooler, lower pressure): selective for terpenes (low BP volatiles), low throughput, "live" terp fraction for re-blending
- **Supercritical CO2** (hotter, higher pressure): pulls cannabinoids efficiently; higher pressure = more lipid co-extraction; default for commercial CO2 oil

**Equipment:**
- SCFE system (Supercritical Fluid Extraction) — $100k-500k+
- High-pressure pumps, condensers, separator vessels
- Often paired with ethanol post-processing for refinement

**Common products:**
- CO2 oil for vape carts (often re-cut with terps because CO2 strips natives)
- Distillate substrate
- Full-spectrum CBD products (hemp industry mainstay)

**Pros/cons:**
- Pro: zero solvent residual (CO2 fully escapes), GRAS-rated, scalable
- Con: capital intensive, slow throughput, often strips terps requiring re-introduction, lipid co-extraction common

### Method 4 — Distillation (Post-Processing, Not Primary Extraction)

**Short-path distillation:** vacuum + heat separates molecules by boiling point. Under vacuum, THC BP ~157°C, terps lower, CBN higher.

**Wiped film evaporator:** continuous-flow industrial version of short path.

**Result:** distillate — clear/amber oil, 85-99% cannabinoid, terps stripped (often re-added later as botanical or cannabis-derived terps).

**Use cases:** vape carts (distillate + terps + cut agent), edibles (purity, taste-neutral), pharmaceutical work.

**Distillate pros/cons:**
- Pro: standardized potency, neutral flavor, purity for formulation
- Con: full-spectrum lost, single-cannabinoid focus, re-added terps often "fake"-tasting

### Method 5 — Isolates and Crystals

- **THCa diamonds:** crystallized THCa from supersaturated terp-rich sauce. Solventless route via diamond mining from cured rosin; solvent route via PHO/BHO sauce cure.
- **CBD isolate:** crystalline CBD from chromatography or crystallization; white powder, 99%+
- **Other isolates:** CBG, CBN, CBC isolates increasingly available via column chromatography

## SAFETY (Non-Negotiable)

### Solventless safety

- Freeze dryer — vacuum implosion risk (rare); pump oil hazard
- Rosin press — burns from heated plates (200°F+); pinch points; hydraulic fluid leaks
- Bubble hash — slip/cold hazards from ice work, cold water exposure
- All solventless = generally low risk

### Solvent safety

**Hydrocarbon:**
- Vapors heavier than air; pool in low spots and ignite
- Flash point well below room temp
- Static spark ignites; non-conductive plastic forbidden
- All operations in C1D1 explosion-proof room with gas monitors, fire suppression, two egress paths
- **Open blasting: never.** Felony in most jurisdictions; lethal frequently.

**Ethanol:**
- Flash point 13°C — flammable
- Vapor accumulation possible
- Lower explosion risk than hydrocarbons but real fire risk
- Ventilated extraction room, no open flames, no sparks

**CO2:**
- High pressure (over 1000 psi typical operating)
- Vessel rupture = projectile risk
- Asphyxiation in confined space (CO2 displaces O2)
- ASME-certified vessels; pressure relief valves; CO2 monitors required

**Residual solvent testing:**
- Gas chromatography at certified lab
- State limits typical: butane <5000ppm, propane <5000ppm, ethanol <5000ppm
- Quality producers: <500ppm butane
- **Untested concentrate = unknown — don't consume.**

## DECISION TREE

```
WHAT'S YOUR GOAL?

→ Full-spectrum, flavor-first, want to do it yourself
   ├── Have flower only → ROSIN (flower)
   ├── Want top-tier → ICE WATER HASH → press to ROSIN (hash rosin)
   └── Top-shelf cure → LIVE ROSIN (fresh-frozen → ice water hash → rosin)

→ Want kief / sprinkle on flower
   └── DRY SIFT (pollen box or tumbler)

→ Want shatter / wax / live resin (commercial style)
   └── HYDROCARBON in licensed lab — not a home project

→ Want clean, scalable, safer-than-hydrocarbon commercial extract
   └── ETHANOL extraction

→ Want pharma-grade purity, single cannabinoid, neutral flavor
   └── DISTILLATION (after CO2 or ethanol primary extraction)

→ Want THCa crystals
   ├── Solventless route → diamond mining from rosin cure
   └── Solvent route → PHO sauce cure (lab)

→ Want oral-active edibles ingredient
   ├── From flower → DECARB → infuse butter/oil
   ├── From rosin → DECARB rosin gently → mix into fat
   └── Commercial → DISTILLATE with known potency

→ Just want to vape clean
   └── ROSIN cart or LIVE RESIN cart (avoid distillate w/ added terps if you can)
```

## COMMON MISCONCEPTION TRAPS

| Belief | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Solventless is automatically safer to consume" | Mostly true, but starting-material contamination still passes through. Test commercial inputs. |
| "Live resin = live rosin" | No. Live resin is hydrocarbon. Live rosin is solventless. Both use fresh-frozen input. |
| "CO2 is solventless" | CO2 IS a solvent — just one that fully evaporates. Marketing often blurs this. |
| "Distillate is the strongest" | Strongest by THC%. NOT strongest by experience — full-spectrum often feels more potent at lower THC due to entourage effect. |
| "Higher temp rosin = more potent" | Just more yield. Mono-terpenes volatilize from ~160°F; sesqui-terpenes hold past 250°F; cannabinoids degrade meaningfully above 220°F. Higher temp = more yield, less terp + cannabinoid integrity. |
| "Six-star hash will always melt" | Melt depends on trichome maturity AND purity. Old hash loses melt over months. |
| "Bigger micron rosin bag = bigger yield" | Up to a point. Beyond 120µ you pull plant matter. 90µ is common sweet spot for flower rosin. |
| "Closed-loop BHO is safe" | Safer than open blasting. Still requires C1D1, gas detection, training, licensing. |
| "Decarb is automatic when smoking" | Yes — combustion handles it. Edibles do NOT — must pre-decarb. |
| "Diamonds are pure THC" | THCa, technically. Decarbs to THC at consumption. Slightly different molecule. |
| "Air-drying bubble hash works fine" | No. Microbial risk + terp loss + melt loss. Freeze dryer is the modern standard. |
| "All hydrocarbon residue is purged out" | Only if purged correctly under vacuum and lab-tested. Untested = unknown. |
